# Full Trip Itinerary

**May 29 – June 9, 2026  •  Family of 5  •  2 adults, 3 kids under 10**

Vibe: Balanced — 2-3 activities per day with rest time.

---

## Must-Have Experiences
- ✅ DisneySea or Disneyland
- ✅ teamLab digital art
- ✅ Shinkansen bullet train ride
- ✅ Ryokan + onsen (Hakone)
- ✅ Traditional temples & shrines
- ✅ Pokemon / Nintendo / Anime

## TOKYO — May 29 to Jun 2 (4 nights)
**Hotel:** MIMARU Tokyo Hatchobori or Station East  
**Neighborhood:** Nihonbashi/Hatchobori — quiet, central, not touristy

### Day 1 — Arrival (Friday, May 29)
- **Afternoon** ✈ Arrive Narita or Haneda. Get Suica IC cards at airport. Limousine bus or Narita Express to central Tokyo.
- **Late afternoon** 🏨 Check into MIMARU. Grab essentials from the konbini downstairs.
- **Evening** 🍜 Easy first dinner in Ginza (10-min walk) — ramen or gyukatsu. Early night.
> **Tip:** Try to stay awake until 8pm local time. Expect 5am wake-ups for a day or two.

### Day 2 — Shibuya & Harajuku (Saturday, May 30)
- **Morning** ⛩ Meiji Shrine — large forested grounds, peaceful, great for kids. 30-45 min.
- **Mid-morning** 🎪 Takeshita Street, Harajuku — colorful crepes, candy, pop culture shops.
- **Lunch** 🍱 Marion Crepes or Kawaii Monster Cafe (book ahead).
- **Afternoon** 🎮 **Pokemon Center Shibuya** ⭐ MUST-DO — enormous, budget 1.5-2 hours. Also visit Nintendo Tokyo in Shibuya Parco (same building).
- **Evening** 🍣 Kura Sushi or Hamazushi (conveyor belt) — huge hit with kids.
> **Tip:** Set a spending limit before entering Pokemon Center. It gets overwhelming fast.

### Day 3 — DisneySea ⭐ MUST-DO (Sunday, May 31)
- **Early morning** 🚂 JR Keiyo Line from Tokyo Station to Maihama (~15 min). Leave by 8am.
- **All day** 🎡 **Tokyo DisneySea** — the best theme park in the world for families.
  - Kids: Sindbad's Storybook Voyage, Mermaid Lagoon (whole indoor kids section), Toy Story Mania
  - Use the Tokyo Disney Resort app for Premier Access (paid skip-the-line)
  - Lunch at Restaurant Sakura at 11:30am before the rush
- **Evening** 🌙 Stay for the Fantasy Springs night show. JR Keiyo Line back afterward.
> **Tip:** Buy tickets at tokyodisneyresort.jp — same-day tickets often sell out. Download the app before leaving home.

### Day 4 — teamLab Planets + Shinjuku (Monday, June 1)
- **Morning** 😴 Slow recovery morning. Konbini breakfast.
- **Late morning** 🌊 **teamLab Planets, Toyosu** ⭐ MUST-DO — immersive digital art, infinity flower room, wade-through water room. Pre-book at planets.teamlab.art. ~1.5-2 hours. Some rooms require bare feet.
- **Lunch** 🍱 Fresh seafood bowls near Toyosu Market.
- **Afternoon** 🌿 Shinjuku Gyoen Garden — beautiful in early June, large lawns for kids.
- **Evening** 🍜 Shinjuku Ramen Street. Ichiran Ramen (individual booths — kids love it).
> **Tip:** Pack bags tonight for Hakone. Arrange takkyubin luggage forwarding from MIMARU to Gora Kadan.

## HAKONE — Jun 2 to Jun 4 (2 nights)
**Ryokan:** Gōra Kadan — former Imperial villa, private onsen, kaiseki meals included  
**Travel:** Shinkansen Tokyo → Odawara (~35 min) then Hakone Tozan Railway to Gora (~55 min)

### Day 5 — Travel to Hakone + Arrival (Tuesday, June 2)
- **Morning** 🚄 **Shinkansen to Odawara** ⭐ MUST-DO (first bullet train ride!) — book window seats, right side for possible Mt. Fuji views.
- **Mid-morning** 🚞 Hakone Tozan Railway — scenic mountain switchback railway. Very fun for kids.
- **Late morning** 🏯 **Check in — Gora Kadan** ⭐ MUST-DO — yukata robes for everyone, private onsen introduction, tatami rooms.
- **Afternoon** ♨️ First private onsen experience. Walk the gardens.
- **Evening** 🍱 Kaiseki dinner — multi-course Japanese feast, served in your room or dining room.
> **Tip:** Put on yukata robes as soon as you arrive and keep them on. Kids love waddling around in them.

### Day 6 — Hakone Exploration (Wednesday, June 3)
- **Morning** 🍳 Traditional Japanese breakfast (grilled fish, miso, tamagoyaki, rice).
- **Mid-morning** 🗻 Hakone Ropeway over Owakudani volcanic valley — steam vents, sulphur smell, possible Mt. Fuji views. Buy kuro-tamago (black eggs boiled in volcanic springs).
- **Midday** 🚢 Lake Ashi sightseeing cruise to Hakone-machi (~40 min). Pirate-ship boats.
- **Lunch** 🍡 Lakefront soba and tempura.
- **Afternoon** 🏛 Hakone Open Air Museum — sculptures, Picasso pavilion, foot spa.
- **Evening** ♨️ Afternoon onsen, second kaiseki dinner.
> **Tip:** June can be hazy for Mt. Fuji. Early morning is best. Owakudani valley is spectacular regardless.

## KYOTO — Jun 4 to Jun 7 (3 nights)
**Hotel:** Six Senses Kyoto — Higashiyama district  
**Travel:** Hakone → Odawara (local train), Odawara → Kyoto (Shinkansen, ~90 min)

### Day 7 — Arrival + Higashiyama Walk (Thursday, June 4)
- **Morning** 🚄 Shinkansen to Kyoto. Forward bags to Six Senses via takkyubin.
- **Early afternoon** 🏨 Check in — Six Senses Kyoto. Bamboo, stone paths, very different energy to Tokyo.
- **Afternoon** ⛩ **Higashiyama Walk** ⭐ MUST-DO — Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka stone-paved lanes, traditional shops, end at Kiyomizudera temple. The most atmospheric street in Japan.
- **Late afternoon** 🍵 Nakamura Tokichi — matcha soft serve, parfaits, wagashi sweets.
- **Evening** 🍣 Dinner in Higashiyama — village-like feel, very different to Tokyo.

### Day 8 — Fushimi Inari + Gion (Friday, June 5)
- **Early morning** ⛩ **Fushimi Inari Taisha** ⭐ MUST-DO — go before 8am for near-empty lower paths. Thousands of vermillion torii gates. 30-45 min lower loop is manageable for kids.
- **Mid-morning** 🦊 Fushimi market stalls — yakitori, inari sushi, soft serve.
- **Lunch** 🍱 Nishiki Market ('Kyoto's Kitchen') — narrow covered market, hundreds of stalls, great for grazing.
- **Afternoon** 🌸 Gion District stroll — Hanamikoji Street, traditional ochaya teahouses, possible maiko spotting 5-6pm.
- **Evening** 🍜 Dinner in Gion — ask Six Senses concierge for a booking.
> **Tip:** Fushimi Inari before 8am is magical — the light through the gates at dawn is extraordinary.

### Day 9 — Arashiyama + Philosopher's Path (Saturday, June 6)
- **Morning** 🎋 Arashiyama Bamboo Grove — taxi (~25 min). Go before 9am. Otherworldly.
- **Mid-morning** 🐒 Tenryuji temple + garden OR Iwatayama Monkey Park (164 wild monkeys — big hit with kids).
- **Lunch** 🍱 Yudofu (tofu hot pot) in Arashiyama.
- **Afternoon** 🛤 Philosopher's Path — 2km canal walk from Ginkakuji to Nanzenji. Very peaceful.
- **Late afternoon** 🛍 Nishiki Market + Teramachi Street for souvenirs. Nintendo store on Teramachi.
- **Evening** 🍱 Splurge farewell Kyoto dinner — ask Six Senses concierge. Mizai or Kikunoi for kaiseki.
> **Tip:** Pack bags tonight and forward to Fraser Residence Nankai in Osaka via takkyubin.

## OSAKA — Jun 7 to Jun 9 (2 nights)
**Hotel:** Fraser Residence Nankai — 2-bedroom suite, directly above Namba Station  
**Travel:** Shinkansen Kyoto → Shin-Osaka (~15 min), then subway to Namba (~20 min)

### Day 10 — Osaka Arrival + Dotonbori (Sunday, June 7)
- **Morning** 🚄 Shinkansen to Osaka — shortest bullet train ride of the trip (15 min).
- **Midday** 🏨 Check in — Fraser Residence Nankai. Step outside and you're in the middle of everything.
- **Afternoon** 🎮 Den Den Town — Osaka's anime/electronics district. Pokemon cards, retro games, figures.
- **Late afternoon** 🏰 Osaka Castle (optional) — iconic exterior, large park for kids.
- **Evening** 🍢 **Dotonbori food crawl** ⭐ MUST-DO — takoyaki, kushikatsu, okonomiyaki, crab, neon signs, giant mechanical crab. Osaka is Japan's food capital.
> **Tip:** Never double-dip kushikatsu sauce — the cardinal rule of Osaka dining.

### Day 11 — Universal Studios or Final Exploration (Monday, June 8)
**Option A — Universal Studios Japan (USJ):**
- Full day at USJ — Wizarding World of Harry Potter, Super Nintendo World (interactive Mario land), Minion Park. Buy Express Pass in advance. Direct subway from Namba (~25 min).

**Option B — Relaxed final day:**
- Kuromon Ichiba Market (fresh seafood, wagyu skewers), Namba Parks, Tsutenkaku tower in Shinsekai.

- **Evening** 🌃 Final dinner — Kani Doraku (crab), Mizuno (okonomiyaki), or concierge booking.
- **Night** 🧳 Pack everything. Forward heavy bags to Tokyo airport via takkyubin (do this the night before).

### Day 12 — Departure (Tuesday, June 9)
- **Morning** 🚄 Shinkansen Shin-Osaka → Tokyo (~2h 30min). Buy an ekiben (station bento) for the journey.
- **Midday** ✈ Transfer to Narita or Haneda. Allow 90 min minimum before departure.

## Practical Info

### Before You Leave Home
| Task | Notes |
|------|-------|
| **JR Pass** | Consider 14-day pass — covers all Shinkansen in this itinerary. Buy at jrpass.com before departure. |
| **DisneySea tickets** | tokyodisneyresort.jp — sells out weeks ahead, especially weekends |
| **teamLab Planets** | planets.teamlab.art — sells out 2-4 weeks ahead in peak season |
| **Gora Kadan** | gorakadan.com — book THIS FIRST, hardest reservation |
| **MIMARU Pokemon room** | mimaruhotels.com — sells out 3-4 months ahead |
| **Japan SIM card** | Buy before departure or at airport (Ubigi, IIJmio, Docomo tourist SIM) |
| **Google Maps** | Download Tokyo, Hakone, Kyoto, Osaka offline maps |
| **Tokyo Disney app** | Set up and link tickets before leaving home |

### Booking Priority
1. 🔴 **Book immediately:** Gora Kadan + MIMARU Pokemon room
2. 🟠 **3-4 months out:** Six Senses Kyoto, Fraser Residence Nankai, other hotels
3. 🟡 **2-3 months out:** DisneySea tickets, teamLab Planets
4. 🟢 **1 month out:** Shinkansen seat reservations, USJ Express Pass
5. 🔵 **2 weeks out:** Special restaurant reservations (Six Senses concierge can help)

### Getting Around
- **Suica/Pasmo card:** Get at airport. Works on all subways, JR trains, buses, konbini purchases.
- **Shinkansen:** Book reserved seats for 5 people. Right side of train heading west for Mt. Fuji views.
- **Taxis:** Safe, clean, metered. Show destination on phone. Uber also works.
- **Luggage forwarding (takkyubin):** ¥1500-2500 per bag. All hotels in this itinerary arrange it. Only carry day bags on travel days.

### Food Tips
- **Kid-safe bets:** Ramen, udon, conveyor belt sushi, katsu curry, yakitori, gyoza, takoyaki, rice dishes
- **Konbini:** 7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson — amazing for breakfast, snacks, and quick meals
- **Must-try:** Ramen, sushi, takoyaki, okonomiyaki, karaage chicken, gyukatsu, matcha everything, ekiben
