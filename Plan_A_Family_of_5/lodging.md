## Tokyo — May 29 to Jun 2 (4 nights)

### ⭐ Top Pick: MIMARU Tokyo Hatchobori / Station East
- **Why:** Purpose-built family apartments — fits 5 in one unit with separate bedrooms, bunk beds for kids, kitchenette, in-building laundry
- **Location:** Nihonbashi/Hatchobori — quiet, central, not touristy (see [`tokyo.md`](./tokyo.md))
- **Rating:** 4.8 / 5
- **Book at:** https://mimaruhotels.com
- **Notes:** Book early — connecting apartment rooms sell out. Station East location is a 10-min walk to Tokyo Station (ideal for Shinkansen departure to Hakone on Jun 2)

### Alternative: Oakwood Premier Tokyo (Marunouchi)
- Full apartment suites, steps from Tokyo Station
- More traditional luxury hotel feel with kitchen + washer/dryer
- https://www.oakwoodpremier.com/tokyo

---

## Hakone — Jun 2 to Jun 4 (2 nights)

### ⭐ Top Pick: Gōra Kadan
- **Why:** One of Japan's most celebrated ryokans, built on grounds of a former Imperial villa. Private onsen baths, kaiseki dinner + breakfast included, tatami rooms, exceptional service
- **Location:** Gōra, Hakone
- **Rating:** 4.5 / 5
- **Book at:** https://www.gorakadan.com
- **Notes:** Book this FIRST — hardest property to get for large rooms. Book the largest suite (Hanare-style cottage) to give the family space. Kids sleep on futons alongside parents.

### Alternative: Hakone Ginyu
- Slightly more intimate, private in-room open-air onsen, mountain views
- https://www.hakoneginyu.co.jp

---

## Kyoto — Jun 4 to Jun 7 (3 nights)

### ⭐ Top Pick: Six Senses Kyoto
- **Why:** Heart of Higashiyama historic district — walk to dozens of temples. Reviewers with toddlers specifically praise staff attentiveness. Exceptional breakfast, beautiful spa.
- **Location:** Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto
- **Rating:** 4.6 / 5
- **Book at:** https://www.sixsenses.com/kyoto
- **Notes:** Walking distance to Fushimi Inari, Arashiyama (taxi), Nishiki Market, Gion district

### Alternative: Park Hyatt Kyoto
- World-class service, same Higashiyama location
- Reviewers note staff memorized their children's preferences by day 2
- https://www.hyatt.com/park-hyatt/itmph-park-hyatt-kyoto

---

## Osaka — Jun 7 to Jun 9 (2 nights)

### ⭐ Top Pick: Fraser Residence Nankai
- **Why:** Two-bedroom suites with 2 bathrooms, full kitchen, in-unit washer/dryer. Steps from Namba Station. Multiple reviewers with families of 5 call out how well the layout works.
- **Location:** Namba, Osaka (above Nankai Namba Station)
- **Rating:** 4.5 / 5
- **Book at:** https://www.frasershospitality.com/fraser-residence-nankai-osaka
- **Notes:** Excellent for the last leg — space + flexibility. Direct Shinkansen connection back to Tokyo from Shin-Osaka Station for Jun 9 departure.

### Alternative: Ritz-Carlton Osaka
- Traditional luxury, Umeda area, families regularly upgraded to larger suites
- https://www.ritzcarlton.com/osaka

---

## Booking Priority Order

1. **Gōra Kadan** (Hakone) — fills up fastest, book immediately
2. **MIMARU Pokémon rooms** (Tokyo) — sells out 3–4 months ahead if desired
3. Six Senses Kyoto
4. Fraser Residence Nankai
5. MIMARU Hatchobori / Station East

## General Tips

- All four top picks can arrange **luggage forwarding (takkyubin)** between cities
- Ask each property about **family room configurations** when booking — mention 2 adults + 3 kids under 10
- Ryokan (Hakone): kids sleep on futons on tatami floor alongside parents — totally normal and fun for kids
