# 🏨 Accommodations

All hotels for the 10-day trip. Group of 6: 2 adults + 2 kids (7 & 9) + grandma (75).

## Tokyo — Hyatt Regency Tokyo (5 nights)

**Dates:** Days 1–5
**Address:** 2-7-2 Nishishinjuku, Shinjuku, Tokyo 160-0023
**Phone:** +81-3-3348-1234
**Website:** [hyatt.com](https://www.hyatt.com/hyatt-regency/en-US/tyoty-hyatt-regency-tokyo)
**Rating:** 4.2/5 (6,000+ reviews)

### Why This Hotel
- Best combination of location, comfort, and practicality for a multi-generational family in Tokyo
- Central Shinjuku — metro, restaurants, and shopping all walkable
- Free shuttle bus to Shinjuku Station (game-changer for grandma on tired days)
- Western beds throughout — grandma comfort ✅
- 7-Eleven convenience store in the basement
- Upper floors have stunning Mt. Fuji views
- Multi-lingual, attentive staff

### Room Booking
- **Book:** 2 connecting twin rooms
- **Request:** Rooms on the same floor, adjacent or connecting if possible
- **Grandma's room:** Request high floor with Mt. Fuji view (she'll love waking up to it)
- **Note:** Rooms are standard Japanese size — not large, but well-designed

### Amenities
- Multiple restaurants on-site (Japanese, French, Chinese)
- Hotel bar and lounge
- Fitness center
- Concierge desk (excellent for restaurant reservations and tips)

### Getting There from Haneda
- **Keikyu Line:** HND → Shinjuku (~30 min, use Suica, ~¥600)
- **Limousine Bus:** HND → Shinjuku (45–60 min, ~¥1,300/person) — better for grandma with luggage

## Hakone — Hakone Kowakien Tenyu (2 nights)

**Dates:** Days 6–7
**Address:** 1297 Ninotaira, Hakone, Kanagawa 250-0407
**Phone:** +81-465-20-0260
**Website:** [ten-yu.com](https://www.ten-yu.com)
**Rating:** 4.3/5 (2,000+ reviews)

### Why This Ryokan
- Premium ryokan with the best onsen experience in Hakone for families
- Rooftop open-air bath ("infinity" style) with dramatic mountain views
- **Swimsuit-friendly onsen** — crucial for kids and self-conscious guests (rentable swimsuits available)
- Dinner and breakfast included — gorgeous multi-course meals
- Men/women rotate baths each day so everyone gets both experiences over 2 nights
- Hotel shuttle from Gora Station
- Family-appropriate atmosphere

### Room Booking
- **Book:** 2 rooms
- **Grandma's room:** Request **Western-style beds** — ryokans often have hybrid options (Western bed but tatami décor)
- **Family room:** Tatami/futon room for the adventurous — kids will LOVE sleeping on futons
- **Request:** Adjacent rooms on same floor

### What's Included
- Kaiseki dinner (multi-course, served in room or private dining)
- Japanese breakfast (traditional with rice, miso, grilled fish, pickles)
- Complimentary yukata robes and slippers for all guests
- Access to all onsen facilities
- Shuttle service from/to Gora Station

### Onsen Tips
- **Before your first onsen:** Brief on etiquette — rinse completely before entering the bath, no swimsuits in traditional areas
- **Swimsuit zone:** Check with hotel which specific baths allow swimsuits — typically the outdoor area
- **Kids in onsen:** Children are welcome but should be supervised; the swimsuit areas are most comfortable for first-timers
- **Tattoo policy:** Most Japanese onsen prohibit visible tattoos — check if relevant

### Getting There
- Romancecar from Shinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto (~85 min) → Hakone Tozan Railway to Gora (~40 min) → hotel shuttle
- Or taxi from Hakone-Yumoto direct to hotel (~15 min, ~¥2,500)

### Nearby
- Hakone Kowakien Yunessun (water park, 5-min walk)
- Hakone Tozan Railway (to Gora, Ropeway access)
- Convenience store nearby for snacks

## Kyoto — Four Seasons Hotel Kyoto (3 nights)

**Dates:** Days 8–10
**Address:** 445-3 Myohorin Maekawachō, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto 605-0932
**Phone:** +81-75-541-8288
**Website:** [fourseasons.com/kyoto](https://www.fourseasons.com/kyoto)
**Rating:** 4.4/5 (1,700+ reviews)

### Why This Hotel
The standout hotel of the trip — and the right choice for your group's final and most culturally rich destination.

- **Location:** Higashiyama district — the most beautiful, traditional part of Kyoto; major temples and the Higashiyama walking path steps from the door
- **800-year-old Shakusui-en pond garden** — stunning, serene, grandma will love having coffee overlooking it
- **Two-Bedroom Heritage Garden Residence** — designed exactly for a group like yours: 2 bedrooms, 2 full bathrooms, large living/dining area, private balcony over the pond garden
- **Largest indoor pool in Kyoto** — kids can swim after temple-heavy days
- **Award-winning spa** — book treatments for grandma
- Service is legendary — staff anticipate every need

### Room Booking
- **Book:** Two-Bedroom Heritage Garden Residence (or 2 adjacent deluxe rooms if the suite is not available)
- **Request through concierge:** Seasonal welcome activity for the kids at check-in
- **Pre-arrival requests:** High chair/step stool if needed, any dietary requirements for restaurant

### Recommended On-Site Services (Pre-Book Through Concierge)
- Spa treatment for grandma (Ishigaki stone massage, Japanese ritual bath)
- In-room tea ceremony experience
- Private morning temple walk with a hotel guide
- Kaiseki dinner reservation at a nearby Michelin-starred restaurant
- Private car transfers (airport, temples, Nishiki Market)

### Amenities
- Brasserie restaurant (Western + Japanese, family-friendly)
- Tōki bar (beautiful design, great cocktails)
- Tea Lounge (matcha, wagashi sweets — grandma heaven)
- Indoor pool + fitness center
- Business center (for printing boarding passes, etc.)

### Location Advantages
- **Walk to:** Kiyomizudera Temple (10 min), Ninenzaka/Sannenzaka lanes (5 min), Maruyama Park (10 min)
- **Easy taxi to:** Fushimi Inari (~15 min), Kinkaku-ji (~20 min), Arashiyama (~25 min), Nishiki Market (~10 min)
- **Higashiyama Scenic Path** starts right outside the hotel

### Getting There from Hakone
- Shinkansen: Odawara → Kyoto (~75 min, covered by JR Pass)
- Taxi from Kyoto Station to hotel (~15 min, ~¥1,500–2,000)
- **Book private car through hotel** for grandma's comfort — hotel can arrange pick-up at Kyoto Station
