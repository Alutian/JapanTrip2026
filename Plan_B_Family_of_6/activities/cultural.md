## MiPig Café — Harajuku 🐷

**Day:** Day 2
**Location:** Near Takeshita Street, Harajuku, Tokyo
**Website:** [mipig.cafe](https://mipig.cafe)
**Book:** Up to 60 days in advance

### What It Is
A micropig café in the heart of Harajuku — one of Tokyo's most beloved and unique animal café experiences. Tiny potbelly pigs roam freely in a bright, clean space while guests interact with them in timed sessions.

### Booking
- Private room sessions available for groups (recommended for 6 people)
- **Cost:** ¥3,300/person
- **Duration:** 25-min standard or 60-min (request longer for a group)
- **Minimum age:** 5+ (kids 7 & 9 are perfect)
- **Book online** — fills up fast, especially weekends

### What to Expect
- Pigs are gentle, curious, and surprisingly clean
- Kids can hold and feed them (feed available at the café)
- Even grandma will be completely charmed
- Great photo opportunities
- Staff speak some English

### Tips
- Book the **private room** — more relaxed than sharing with strangers
- Visit **Takeshita Street first** (right next door), then walk to MiPig
- Don't wear expensive clothing — pigs are friendly but pigs are pigs

## Fushimi Inari Taisha — Kyoto ⛩️

**Day:** Day 9 (7:00am)
**Address:** 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi Ward, Kyoto
**Website:** [inari.jp](https://inari.jp)
**Entry:** Free | **Hours:** Open 24 hours
**Getting there:** JR Nara Line from Kyoto Station to Inari Station (~5 min) — exit is literally at the shrine gate

### What It Is
The iconic Shinto shrine famous for its thousands of vermillion torii gates that form tunnels stretching up Mount Inari. One of the most photographed places in Japan — and one of the most genuinely atmospheric.

### Early Morning Visit (Essential)
- Go at **7:00am** — nearly empty, ethereal morning light
- By 10am it's crowded with tour groups
- Early morning is also spiritually appropriate — you're visiting a functioning religious site

### The Route

**Lower section (30–45 min, suitable for grandma):**
- The main torii tunnel from the entrance up to the first shrine gate
- Most iconic photography spots in this section
- Flat-ish walk, well-maintained stone paths
- Grandma can enjoy the most beautiful stretch without the full mountain

**Mid section (1–1.5 hr round trip):**
- Continue up to Yotsutsuji intersection (~30 min from bottom)
- Spectacular view over Kyoto city
- Good turnaround point for families with young kids

**Full hike (2–3 hours):**
- Summit and all four sub-shrines
- Steep in places
- Kids 7 & 9 can do this if they're energetic

### Practical Notes
- **Stalls** sell snacks and drinks at the base — get yakitori (grilled skewers) before the climb
- Stone paths — **wear appropriate shoes** (grandma especially)
- Can be muddy after rain — bring umbrella or poncho (June rainy season)
- Photography: The torii are endlessly photogenic — bring a good camera

## Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion) — Kyoto

**Day:** Day 9 (~10:30am)
**Address:** 1 Kinkakujicho, Kita Ward, Kyoto
**Entry:** ¥500/person | **Hours:** 9:00am – 5:00pm
**Getting there:** Taxi from Fushimi Inari (~30 min, ~¥2,500) recommended for grandma

### What It Is
A Zen Buddhist temple covered in gold leaf, reflected in a perfect mirror pond. One of Japan's most iconic images — and one that lives up to the hype even amid crowds.

### Tips
- Arrive by 10:30am — gets progressively more crowded through the day
- **Grounds are fully flat** — excellent for grandma
- The route is circular — takes 30–45 min at a leisurely pace
- Exit through the garden to the small tea house — matcha tea and sweets on the terrace (grandma will love this)
- Photography: East shore of the pond gives the classic shot

## Arashiyama — Kyoto 🎋

**Day:** Day 9 (~1:30pm)
**Getting there:** Taxi from Kinkaku-ji (~20 min) or JR Sagano Line from Kyoto Station

### Bamboo Grove
**Address:** Ukyo Ward, Arashiyama, Kyoto
**Entry:** Free | **Hours:** Always open

- Walk through towering bamboo — atmospheric, otherworldly, and genuinely magical
- The path is short (~10–15 min walk through) but memorable
- Combine with the surrounding temple area rather than bamboo-only

### Tenryu-ji Temple & Garden
**Address:** 68 Susukinobabacho, Sagatenryuji, Ukyo Ward
**Entry:** ¥500 (garden) / ¥800 (garden + interior)
**Hours:** 8:30am – 5:30pm

- One of Japan's finest Zen gardens — UNESCO World Heritage Site
- **Shakusui-style garden** (borrowed scenery) — the mountains behind become part of the composition
- Grandma can sit in the garden while kids explore the surrounding paths
- Large koi pond, raked gravel areas, perfectly maintained

### Katsura River & Togetsu-kyo Bridge
- Flat riverside walk
- The bridge with mountain backdrop is iconic
- **Riverside lunch nearby** — many excellent restaurants with water views
- Recommended: **Yoshida-ya** (traditional yudofu, river view) or any restaurant with an open terrace

### Rickshaw Tour (Optional)
- **Jinrikisha** (rickshaw) tours available throughout Arashiyama
- Can carry 1–2 passengers each
- Fun for kids, romantic for adults, easy for grandma — no walking required
- ~¥4,000–8,000 for a 30-min tour

## Nishiki Market — Kyoto 🍱

**Day:** Day 8 (afternoon)
**Address:** Nishiki, Nakagyo Ward, Kyoto (near Kawaramachi Station)
**Hours:** Most stalls 9am–6pm (some close Wednesday)
**Entry:** Free

### What It Is
"Kyoto's Kitchen" — a 400-meter covered shopping street with 100+ stalls selling Kyoto's finest food products, fresh ingredients, and prepared foods.

### Strategy
- **Graze your way through** — try a few bites at each stall rather than sitting down
- Walk the full length first, then go back for favorites
- Keep free hands — avoid buying bulky items at the start

### Must-Try Foods
| Food | What It Is |
|------|-----------|
| Tsukemono (pickles) | Kyoto's signature — try the miso-pickled cucumber |
| Tamagoyaki | Sweet rolled egg omelette on a stick |
| Yuba | Tofu skin — delicate and uniquely Kyoto |
| Nama-fu | Decorative wheat gluten sweets — beautiful and tasty |
| Matcha soft serve | Best matcha soft serve you'll ever have |
| Dashi-maki tamago | Soft egg roll with dashi broth |
| Kyoto wagyu samples | Some vendors offer small wagyu beef tastes |

### Shopping
- Buy small food gifts here: packaged pickles, matcha products, Japanese sweets (wagashi)
- Best souvenir shopping in Kyoto

### Grandma Comfort
- Covered (rain-proof), flat, no hills
- Benches and rest areas available
- Air circulation is good
- Stalls are easy to browse without standing long

## Gion District Evening Walk — Kyoto 🏮

**Day:** Day 8 (evening, ~6:00–8:30pm)
**Location:** Gion, Higashiyama Ward, Kyoto
**Getting there:** 10-min taxi from Four Seasons (or pleasant walk through Higashiyama path)

### Key Streets
- **Hanamikoji Street** — the main geisha district, stone-paved, lantern-lit
- **Shirakawa Canal** — willow trees lining a narrow canal, fairy-tale beautiful in the evening
- **Shimbashi** — Gion's most traditional street, ochaya teahouses from feudal-era Japan

### Spotting Maiko & Geiko
- Most active: **6:00–9:00pm** as they travel to dinner engagements
- Best areas: Hanamikoji Street and surrounding alleyways
- **Etiquette:** Do not approach, touch, or block their path. Do not photograph without permission (or from very close range). They are working professionals, not tourist attractions.
- Sightings are not guaranteed but common in this neighborhood

### Dinner Options in Gion
- **Tempura Yoshikawa** (excellent tempura, family-friendly)
- **Gion Kappa** (casual, izakaya-style, great for families)
- Ask Four Seasons concierge for kaiseki reservation for this evening if you want a more formal dinner

## Kaiseki Dinner — Kyoto 🍽️

**Day:** Day 9 (evening)
**Book:** 1–2 months in advance through Four Seasons concierge

### What Is Kaiseki?
Japan's highest culinary tradition — a multi-course meal (typically 8–12 courses) where each dish showcases seasonal ingredients through precise technique and beautiful presentation. The Japanese equivalent of haute cuisine, but deeply rooted in Zen Buddhist philosophy of simplicity and seasonality.

### What to Expect
- 8–12 small courses over 2–2.5 hours
- Each course is a work of art in presentation
- Seasonal (June): ingredients like lily bulb, fresh bamboo shoots, ayu (sweetfish), firefly squid
- Served in a private tatami room (zashiki) for your group — intimate and memorable

### Cost
- ¥15,000–30,000/person for a proper kaiseki
- Budget ¥90,000–180,000 for the group
- Worth it — this is the culinary centerpiece of the trip

### Tips for Kids at Kaiseki
- Call ahead and ask if they can prepare a **simplified children's set** (most kaiseki restaurants accommodate this)
- Private room makes it much easier with young kids — no need to worry about other diners
- The presentation will fascinate kids even if they don't eat everything
- Teach the kids beforehand: "itadakimasu" before eating, "gochisosama deshita" after

### Recommendations
Ask Four Seasons concierge — they know which kaiseki restaurants near Gion welcome families, have private rooms, and can accommodate dietary needs.

## Hakone Onsen Experience 🌊

**Day:** Days 6 & 7

### The Etiquette (Brief Guide)
For first-time onsen visitors:

1. **Before entering:** Remove all clothing and place in the basket/locker
2. **Wash first:** Sit at a shower station and scrub completely before entering the communal bath
3. **No swimsuits** in traditional onsen areas (some areas like Yunessun allow them — check signage)
4. **Quiet atmosphere** — speak softly, no splashing
5. **No phone/camera** in bathing areas
6. **Tattoo policy:** Most onsen prohibit visible tattoos — check when booking
7. **Duration:** 10–15 minutes per soak is typical; exit if feeling dizzy

### Kids in Onsen
- Children are welcome at Hakone Kowakien Tenyu
- The swimsuit-allowed outdoor areas are most comfortable for young kids
- Keep visits shorter for kids — overheating can happen
- Make it fun: wear the yukata robes, shuffle around in the wooden sandals

### Health Benefits
- The mineral-rich volcanic water is said to be excellent for:
  - Aching muscles (great after days of walking)
  - Skin
  - Relaxation and sleep quality
  - Joint stiffness (excellent for grandma!)
