# 🎮 Pokémon Experiences

**Day:** Day 4
**For:** Kids (and secretly the adults too)

Three Pokémon experiences on this trip — a full immersion day your kids will talk about for years.

## 1. PokéPark KANTO at Yomiuriland

**Address:** Yomiuriland, 1-4015-1 Yanokuchi, Inagi, Tama Ward, Tokyo 206-8725
**Website:** [pokepark-kanto.co.jp](https://www.pokepark-kanto.co.jp)
**Hours:** 10:00am – 6:00pm (confirm closer to trip)
**Day:** Day 4 morning/afternoon

### What It Is
Opened February 5, 2026 — the world's first permanent outdoor Pokémon theme park, built inside Yomiuriland amusement park in the Tama Hills west of Tokyo. 26,000 square meters of full Pokémon immersion.

### Areas

**Pokémon Forest**
- 500-meter outdoor trail
- 600+ Pokémon "living" in their natural habitats
- Walk through and spot Pokémon in trees, ponds, and bushes
- Download the park app to interact with Pokémon via your phone

**Sedge Town**
- Main village area with attractions, shops, and food
- Attractions include rides and interactive experiences
- **Sedge Gym Show** — a battle performance (~40 min) — book a time slot through the app in advance
- Pokémon merchandise stores
- Photo opportunities with life-size Pokémon

### Tips
- **Allow 3–5 hours minimum** (longer if the kids want multiple ride sessions)
- **Download the PokéPark KANTO app** before visiting — used for booking time slots and interactive features
- The park is part of Yomiuriland — you enter through the amusement park to reach PokéPark
- Grandma option: Yomiuriland has excellent observation areas, seating, and views of the Tama hills — a pleasant place to rest

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: Tickets
**Tickets go on sale exactly 2 months before your visit date at 6:00pm JST.**

This means:
- If you plan to visit June 10: tickets on sale **April 10 at 6:00pm JST**
  - = April 10 at 2:00am PST
  - = April 10 at 5:00am EST
- **Set a phone alarm for the exact time.** They sell out within hours.
- Buy 6 tickets immediately when they go on sale

### Getting There
- **From Shinjuku:** ~25–30 minutes by train
- Keio Line from Shinjuku → Keio-Yomiuriland Station
- Then take the **gondola lift** up to Yomiuriland (~5 min, beautiful views)
- Or: Odakyu Line to Yomiuriland-mae, then bus

## 2. Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO

**Address:** Sunshine City, 3F, 3-1-1 Higashi-Ikebukuro, Toshima, Tokyo
**Location:** Inside Sunshine City mall, Ikebukuro
**Hours:** 10:00am – 8:00pm (typically)
**Website:** [pokemon.co.jp](https://www.pokemon.co.jp/gp/pokecen/english/)
**Day:** Day 4 afternoon

### What It Is
The largest Pokémon Center in Japan — a massive retail experience spanning approximately 650 tsubo (~2,100 sq ft) with over 2,500 different Pokémon items. This is the flagship.

### What's Inside
- **Main store:** Every Pokémon merchandise category imaginable — plush, cards, clothing, accessories, stationery, homeware, tech accessories, figures
- **Pikachu Sweets by Pokémon Café:** Takeout-style café with seasonal drinks in collectible character sleeves, themed pastries and snacks
- **Exclusive items:** MEGA TOKYO has Ikebukuro-exclusive merchandise not found at other Pokémon Centers

### Shopping Tips
- **Set a budget before entering** — for each kid and for the adults too
- Best items to buy here: plush Pokémon (excellent quality), card packs (Japanese cards are a collectible), Pikachu sweets
- Some items sell out — popular plush of current generation Pokémon go fast
- Check [pokemon.co.jp](https://www.pokemon.co.jp) for current stock and special events

### For Grandma During Pokémon Center Visit
Sunshine City is a large, comfortable, air-conditioned shopping mall:
- Food court with excellent variety
- Multiple coffee shops (Starbucks, Japanese chains)
- Department store section
- **Sunshine Aquarium** on the rooftop (if she wants an activity)
- Comfortable seating areas throughout

### Getting There from Yomiuriland
- Train to Ikebukuro Station (~20–30 min depending on route)
- Sunshine City is a 5-min walk from the east exit of Ikebukuro Station

## 3. Pokémon Café Tokyo (Check Reopening Date)

**Status:** Closed for renovation as of March 23, 2026 — expected to reopen June 2026
**Location:** Nihombashi Takashimaya, Chuo Ward, Tokyo
**Website:** [pokemoncafe.net](https://www.pokemoncafe.net/en/)

### What It Is
A themed restaurant where Pokémon characters appear on food and drinks — pancakes shaped like Pikachu faces, latte art, character-shaped dishes. A beloved Tokyo experience for Pokémon fans.

### To-Do
- Check reopening date 4–6 weeks before your trip
- If open: **book reservations immediately** (they fill up 1 month in advance)
- Reservations typically available online at pokemoncafe.net

### If It's Not Open
- Don't worry — PokéPark KANTO + Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO is an incredible Pokémon day regardless
- Alternative: **Pokémon Café at DisneySea area hotels** (check availability)

## Pokémon General Tips for Japan

- **Japan-exclusive cards:** Japanese Pokémon cards are cheaper and often have beautiful exclusive art — great to buy for the kids' collections
- **Other Pokémon Centers in Tokyo:** There are several (Tokyo Station, Shibuya, Shinjuku) but MEGA TOKYO is the best
- **Pokémon GO:** Japan has many rare regional Pokémon — your kids might enjoy playing while exploring
- **Character merchandise:** Pikachu, Eevee, and the current generation Pokémon (Paldea starters) tend to have the most product variety
