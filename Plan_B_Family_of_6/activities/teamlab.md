## What Is teamLab Borderless?

A world-renowned immersive digital art museum now in its spectacular new home at Azabudai Hills in central Tokyo. 75+ works of digital art fill a labyrinthine space where artworks flow seamlessly from room to room — there are no maps, no fixed paths, no boundaries between art pieces.

**Perfect for every member of your group:**
- **Kids:** Interactive light rooms, colorful immersive environments, they can touch and interact with many installations
- **Dad:** The technology behind it is mind-blowing — generative art systems running in real-time
- **Grandma:** All exhibits on ONE floor, moving at your own pace, benches and rest areas throughout
- **You:** Beautiful, quiet, meditative in some rooms — a wonderful contrast to the busy theme park days

## Highlights to Look For

**Note:** Artworks rotate and evolve — these are some consistent favorites:

- **Athletics Forest** — interactive installation kids go wild in; running and jumping creates visual effects
- **Crystal Universe** — millions of LED lights form a universe you walk through
- **Floating Flower Garden** — flowers that rise and fall around you as you move
- **Wander through the Crystal World**
- **Tea House** — a unique interactive tea experience (separate booking may be required)
- **Continuous Life in the Bamboo Forest of Phenomena** — hauntingly beautiful

---

## Tips for Your Visit

- **Wear comfortable shoes** — you walk on various surfaces including some uneven areas
- **Wear dark/neutral clothing** — the light art shows up more beautifully on neutral clothes
- **Put phones in portrait mode** — the space is tall; horizontal photos miss the height
- **Don't rush** — some rooms are best experienced by slowing down and sitting/standing still
- **Separate and reconnect** — the space is labyrinthine; set a meeting point and let everyone explore at their own pace
- Kids might get overwhelmed in some darker, more intense rooms — that's okay, move to the next one
- The **Athletics Forest** is great for burning off kids' energy

## After teamLab: Azabudai Hills

The museum is inside Azabudai Hills — one of Tokyo's most stunning new urban developments (opened 2023). It's worth spending 30–60 min exploring after your visit:

- **Dining:** Excellent restaurants at multiple price points — Japanese, Italian, French, casual
- **Comme'N Kids Bakery** — a world-first children's bakery with its own child-sized entrance
- **Garden level:** Beautiful outdoor garden terrace
- **Design shops and galleries**
- The entire complex is architecturally spectacular — dad will love it

### Dinner Recommendation at Azabudai Hills
After an afternoon at teamLab, this is a great place to have dinner before heading back to Shinjuku. The complex has 50+ dining options across all styles and budgets.

## teamLab Planets (Bonus Option — Day 10)

If your group wants a second teamLab experience on departure day, **teamLab Planets** in Toyosu is a completely different experience:

**Address:** 6-1-16 Toyosu, Koto, Tokyo
**Website:** [teamlab.art/jp/e/planets](https://www.teamlab.art/jp/e/planets/)

- Smaller, more intimate than Borderless
- Key difference: **you walk through knee-deep water** into a giant infinity mirror space
- Barefoot throughout (shoes stored at entrance)
- Allow 60–90 minutes
- Kids absolutely lose their minds in the water room
- Book timed entry in advance

> Best as a quick morning activity on Day 10 before heading to the airport, if your flight is in the late afternoon/evening.
