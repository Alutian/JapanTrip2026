# 🏔️ Hakone — Days 6–7

**Stay:** Hakone Kowakien Tenyu (2 nights)
**Address:** 1297 Ninotaira, Hakone, Ashigarashimo District, Kanagawa
**Website:** [ten-yu.com](https://www.ten-yu.com)
**Phone:** +81 465-20-0260

---

## Why Hakone Kowakien Tenyu

- Premium ryokan (Japanese inn) with Western-friendly touches
- Rooftop open-air onsen with mountain views — one of Hakone's best
- **Swimsuit-friendly onsen** — critical for families with kids and self-conscious guests
- Dinner and breakfast included (kaiseki multi-course meals)
- Shuttle service from Gora station
- Hybrid room options with Western beds available — ask when booking for grandma's comfort
- Private onsen sessions can be rented by the family

### Room Booking Notes
- Book **two rooms** for the group of 6
- Request: one room with **Western-style beds** for grandma
- Tatami/futon rooms available for the adventurous (kids will love this)
- Ask about **family-adjacent rooms** or connecting options

## Getting to Hakone from Tokyo

### Option 1: Romancecar Express (Recommended ⭐)
- **Route:** Shinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto (~85 min, no transfers)
- Reserved seats — scenic, comfortable, kids love it
- Book in advance at [odakyu.jp](https://www.odakyu.jp/english/romancecar/)
- Cost: ~¥1,270/person + limited express surcharge (~¥900)
- From Hakone-Yumoto: take ryokan shuttle or local Hakone Tozan train to Gora

### Option 2: Shinkansen + Transfer
- Shinjuku → Odawara (JR, ~35 min) → Hakone-Yumoto → Kowakien
- Faster but less scenic; covered by JR Pass to Odawara

### Luggage Tip
- Send bags ahead via **takkyubin (Yamato Transport)** the morning of Day 6
- Ship from Hyatt Regency directly to Tenyu
- Cost: ~¥1,500–2,000/bag
- Bags arrive same day or next day — you travel light on the train

## Day 6 — Arrive Hakone + Onsen Evening

**Depart Tokyo:** 10:00am
**Arrive Hakone:** ~1:00pm

### Afternoon
- Check into Tenyu
- Receive yukata robes — change into them immediately (this is part of the experience!)
- Explore the hotel grounds
- **First onsen session** — rooftop open-air bath
  - Men and women use separate baths (alternates each day, so both sides experienced over 2 nights)
  - Swimsuit allowed in designated areas — great for kids and less confident bathers
  - Best time: late afternoon before dinner for a quiet experience

### Evening
- **Included kaiseki dinner** — multi-course traditional Japanese feast
  - Seasonal ingredients, beautiful presentation
  - Staff usually serve in room or private dining area
  - Kids note: let hotel know about any dietary restrictions when booking
- Evening: night onsen (magical under the stars)
- Early bed — this is a restorative night

> 💡 This is the trip's exhale moment. No schedule, no rushing. Grandma will be in heaven.

## Day 7 — Ropeway + Yunessun Water Park

### Morning Option A: Hakone Ropeway (Family Adventure)
**Grandma option: relaxed morning at ryokan**

- 9:00am — Take Hakone Tozan Cable Car from Gora → Sounzan (~10 min)
- Board **Hakone Ropeway** → soar over **Owakudani**
  - Active volcanic valley — sulfur vents, lunar landscape, dramatic views
  - On clear June mornings, spectacular Mt. Fuji views
  - Kids absolutely love the gondola experience
- Stop at **Owakudani station:** try **kuro tamago** (black hard-boiled eggs cooked in volcanic springs — legend says each egg adds 7 years to your life 🥚)
- Continue ropeway to **Togendai** on Lake Ashi
- Board **sightseeing boat** across Lake Ashi
  - Famous view: **torii gate rising from the water** with Mt. Fuji backdrop
  - ~30 min scenic cruise
- Return by bus or taxi to Kowakien (~20 min)

### Morning Option B (grandma stays)
- Hotel breakfast at leisure
- Spa treatment at Tenyu
- Sit in the hotel garden
- Light walk around the hotel grounds

### Afternoon: Yunessun Water Park
**Location:** Adjacent to Kowakien Tenyu (short walk or hotel shuttle)
**Website:** [yunessun.com](https://www.yunessun.com/english/)

Two connected areas:

**Yunessun (swimsuit zone) — for families**
- Outdoor slides and pools
- Cave bath and waterfall experience
- Wine bath, coffee bath, green tea bath (novelty baths — fun for adults)
- Kids can splash freely
- Grandma can enjoy the warm gentle pools

**Mori no Yu (traditional onsen zone) — no swimsuit**
- Adults-only relaxation
- Quieter, traditional onsen experience
- Good for a split: one adult with kids in Yunessun, one adult in Mori no Yu

**Tickets:** ~¥2,500 adults / ¥1,400 children (swimsuit zone)
Swimsuits, towels, and lockers available to rent

### Evening
- Return to Tenyu for included dinner (second night)
- **Switch onsen sides** — the hotel alternates men/women each day so everyone experiences both baths over the two nights
- Final onsen soak — savor it

## Hakone Practical Notes

### Weather in June
- Early June can be rainy (tsuyu season) — pack light rain gear
- Humidity is high but temperatures are mild (~18–22°C / 64–72°F)
- Mt. Fuji views: best in the early morning on clear days. Don't be discouraged if cloudy.

### Transportation in Hakone
- **Hakone Freepass** — covers all Hakone transportation (ropeway, buses, trains, boat) for 2 days
  - Buy at Shinjuku Station before departure
  - Adults ~¥6,100 from Shinjuku / Children ~¥1,100
  - Excellent value for Day 7's activities
- Taxis available throughout for grandma on longer distances

### What to Buy in Hakone
- **Yosegi-zaiku** (traditional Hakone marquetry woodwork) — puzzle boxes, trays, ornaments
- Hakone specialty sweets
- Onsen-themed souvenirs

### Grandma Comfort in Hakone
- The ryokan staff are exceptionally attentive — don't hesitate to ask for help
- Room service available if she prefers dinner in her room on tired nights
- Shuttle service between hotel areas means minimal walking required
- The hot spring water is said to be therapeutic for joints and muscles
