## Getting to Kyoto from Hakone

**Route:** Hakone Kowakien Tenyu → Odawara Station → Kyoto Station → Four Seasons

1. Hotel shuttle or taxi to **Odawara Station** (~20 min)
2. **Shinkansen (Nozomi/Hikari):** Odawara → Kyoto (~70–80 min)
   - Book reserved seats in advance — family of 6 needs a block of seats
   - Covered partially by JR Pass (Odawara → Kyoto covered)
3. Taxi or private car from Kyoto Station to Four Seasons (~15 min, ~¥2,000)
   - **Book private car through hotel concierge** for grandma's comfort — much easier than navigating Kyoto buses with luggage

> 💡 Send luggage via **takkyubin** from Tenyu to Four Seasons the morning of Day 8. Arrive at hotel with hands free.

## Day 8 — Arrive Kyoto + Nishiki Market + Gion

**Arrive Kyoto:** ~1:00pm

### Afternoon: Nishiki Market
**Location:** Nakagyo Ward, Kyoto (5-min walk from Kawaramachi Station)
**Hours:** Most stalls open 9am–6pm (some close Wednesday)

- Covered shopping street — **rain-proof and flat** (ideal for grandma)
- 100+ stalls selling Kyoto's finest food: fresh tofu, pickled vegetables, tamagoyaki, mochi, matcha everything
- Strategy: **graze your way through** rather than sit-down meals
- Must try: **kyoto pickles (tsukemono)**, **yudofu** (hot tofu), **nama-fu** (wheat gluten sweets), **matcha soft serve**
- Great for buying small gifts and food souvenirs
- Allow 60–90 minutes

### Evening: Gion District Walk
**Best time:** 6:00–8:30pm (dusk and early evening)

- **Hanamikoji Street** — the main geisha street, lantern-lit, atmospheric
- **Shirakawa Canal** — willow trees lining a narrow canal, fairy-tale beautiful
- **Shimbashi** — Gion's most picturesque street, lined with traditional ochaya teahouses
- Keep eyes open for **maiko** (apprentice geisha) or **geiko** (geisha) walking to appointments — most active 6–9pm in Gion
- Be respectful: don't photograph them without permission, don't touch or block their path
- Dinner: **Gion Kappa** (casual, family-friendly) or ask Four Seasons concierge for a reservation at a kaiseki restaurant for this evening

## Day 9 — Temples + Arashiyama 🎋

**Depart hotel:** 7:00am (early — beat the crowds)

### 7:00am: Fushimi Inari Taisha ⛩️
**Address:** 68 Fukakusa Yabunouchicho, Fushimi Ward
**Entry:** Free, open 24 hours

- Go at **sunrise** — nearly empty, magical light through the torii gates
- **What it is:** Thousands of vermillion torii gates forming tunnels up Mt. Inari
- **For grandma:** The lower section (first 10–15 min of walking) is flat and stunning — she can enjoy the most photogenic stretch without the full climb
- **For kids:** Let them run ahead up the mountain; the trail continues for 2–3 hours if you want the full hike
- **Family photo tip:** The first tunnel section at the base is most photogenic and least crowded at 7am
- Allow 45–60 min for the lower section; 2–3 hours for the full mountain hike

### 10:30am: Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)
**Address:** 1 Kinkakujicho, Kita Ward
**Entry:** ¥500/person
**Hours:** 9:00am–5:00pm

- The gilded Zen temple reflected in the mirror pond — unmissable
- **Flat grounds, completely accessible** for grandma
- Allow 30–45 minutes
- Gets very crowded by 11am — arrive as early as possible
- Photography note: the iconic view is from the east shore of the pond

**Transit:** Taxi from Fushimi Inari → Kinkaku-ji (~30 min, ~¥2,500) — worth it for grandma rather than navigating buses

### 1:30pm: Arashiyama 🎋
**Transit:** Taxi from Kinkaku-ji (~20 min) or JR San-in Line (Kyoto → Saga-Arashiyama, ~15 min)

**Bamboo Grove**
- One of Japan's most famous and atmospheric walks
- The towering bamboo creates an eerie, peaceful tunnel of green
- 10–15 min walk through the grove
- Can get crowded midday — don't worry, it's still magical

**Tenryu-ji Temple Garden**
- One of Japan's finest Zen gardens, designated a UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Grandma can sit in the garden while kids explore the surrounding paths
- Koi pond, raked gravel, perfectly composed mountain backdrop
- Entry: ¥500/person (garden only)

**Katsura River + Togetsu-kyo Bridge**
- Iconic bridge over the river with mountain backdrop
- Riverside lunch nearby: many excellent restaurants with river views
- Recommended: **Yoshida-ya** or any riverside restaurant for yudofu or kaiseki lunch

**Optional adds (if energy allows):**
- **Jojakko-ji Temple** — quieter, mossy stone steps, beautiful in any weather
- **Rental rickshaw** — fun for the kids and easy for grandma!

### Evening: Kaiseki Dinner 🍽️
**Book 1–2 months in advance through Four Seasons concierge**

- The meal of the trip — traditional multi-course Japanese haute cuisine
- Each course a small, exquisite dish: seasonal ingredients, perfect technique, beautiful presentation
- Recommended areas: Gion, Pontocho, Higashiyama
- Budget: ¥15,000–30,000/person for a proper kaiseki
- Tip: ask for a private room (zashiki) for the group — your kids will find it easier to manage in a private tatami room
- Kid-friendly: most kaiseki restaurants can prepare a simplified set for children if asked when booking

## Kyoto Practical Notes

### Getting Around Kyoto
- **Taxis** — most practical for a group of 6 with grandma; rarely more than ¥2,000–3,000
- **Kyoto City Bus** — comprehensive but crowded in tourist season
- **JR trains** — useful for Arashiyama (JR Saga-Arashiyama), Fushimi Inari (JR Inari station — literally at the gate)
- **Walk** — Higashiyama district (near the hotel) is highly walkable with excellent temples along a single historic path

### Higashiyama Walking Path (near hotel)
The Four Seasons is perfectly placed for the **Higashiyama Scenic Path** — a stone-paved pedestrian street lined with traditional shops, temples, and cafés. Walk it in the morning or evening for one of Japan's most beautiful streetscapes:
- Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka (stone-paved lanes)
- Kiyomizudera Temple (10-min walk from hotel)
- Maruyama Park

### Weather in June Kyoto
- Warmer than Tokyo (~24–28°C / 75–82°F)
- Rainy season: light showers common — carry fold-up umbrellas
- Humidity is higher than Tokyo — light, breathable clothing essential

### Food Must-Tries in Kyoto
- **Kaiseki** (the city's signature cuisine — book in advance)
- **Yudofu** (hot tofu hot pot — Arashiyama has excellent yudofu restaurants)
- **Matcha everything** — soft serve, parfaits, tea, sweets
- **Kyoto pickles (tsukemono)** from Nishiki Market
- **Obanzai** (Kyoto-style small dishes) — look for obanzai restaurants in Gion
- **Kyoto-style ramen** (lighter, more delicate than Tokyo ramen)

### Grandma Comfort in Kyoto
- Four Seasons concierge is the secret weapon — use them for everything
- **Private rickshaw** in Arashiyama is romantic and requires no walking
- **Taxi everywhere** — Kyoto taxis are cheap, plentiful, and immaculately clean
- Avoid **Kiyomizudera Temple** if her knees are bad — it involves a steep stone staircase
- The hotel garden (800-year-old Shakusui-en pond) is beautiful for a quiet morning sit
