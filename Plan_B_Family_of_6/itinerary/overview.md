## Pacing Philosophy

- **Slow mornings every day** — breakfast at hotel, depart no earlier than 9–10am
- **Grandma's comfort is the floor** — every day has rest options built in
- **Split-up strategy** — on high-energy days, grandma can stay/rest while the family explores
- **Luggage forwarding (takkyubin)** — ship bags between Tokyo → Hakone → Kyoto so nobody drags suitcases on trains

---

## Day 1 — Arrive Tokyo ✈️

**Theme:** Land, recover, settle in
**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo, Shinjuku (check-in)

- Land at **Haneda (HND)**
- Take limousine bus or Keikyu Line to Shinjuku
- Pick up **Suica IC cards** at the airport for everyone (essential for all train/metro travel)
- Check into Hyatt Regency — request connecting twin rooms
- Light neighborhood walk around Shinjuku; konbini dinner
- **Early bed — jet lag is real, especially for grandma and kids**

> 💡 No agenda today. The goal is rest.

---

## Day 2 — Harajuku + MiPig Café 🐷

**Theme:** Kawaii culture, animal café
**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo

- 10:00am — **Takeshita Street, Harajuku** — crepes, capsule toys, kawaii shops, cosplay
- Dad option: explore **Omotesando** (design-forward boutiques and architecture) while grandma rests at a café
- 1:00pm — **MiPig Café** (pre-booked, 60-min private room session) — micropig café, kids 7+ perfect for this
- Afternoon — stroll **Omotesando** together
- Evening — ramen or izakaya dinner in Shinjuku, easy walk back to hotel

> ⚠️ **Book MiPig up to 60 days in advance.** Private room: 3,300 yen/person. [mipig.cafe](https://mipig.cafe)

---

## Day 3 — Tokyo DisneySea 🎡

**Theme:** Full theme park day
**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo

- 8:00am — Depart hotel early
- 8:30am — Arrive DisneySea (JR Keiyo Line from Shinjuku → Maihama, ~50 min)
- **Open to close** — 9:00am–9:00pm
- DisneySea 25th Anniversary "Sparkling Jubilee" in full swing during June visit
- Download **Tokyo Disney Resort App** before arrival — essential for Premier Access and wait times
- Kids focus: Mermaid Lagoon, Sinbad's Storybook Voyage, 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea
- Adults/grandma: Arabian Coast shows, Mediterranean Harbor, restaurants, gorgeous scenery
- Pre-book **Premier Access** for top rides through the app

> ⚠️ **Buy tickets in advance** at tokyodisneyresort.jp — they sell out. Wheelchair rental available inside park for grandma if needed.

---

## Day 4 — PokéPark KANTO + Pokémon Center 🎮

**Theme:** Pokémon immersion day
**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo

- 10:00am — **PokéPark KANTO at Yomiuriland** (25 min from Shinjuku by train)
  - World's first permanent outdoor Pokémon park (opened Feb 2026)
  - 26,000 sqm with Pokémon Forest trail (600+ Pokémon), Sedge Town, rides, Gym show
  - Plan 3–4 hours minimum
  - Grandma option: Yomiuriland park has beautiful views and gentle seating areas
- 3:00pm — **Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO** (Sunshine City, Ikebukuro)
  - Largest Pokémon Center in Japan — 2,500+ items
  - Pikachu Sweets café inside
  - Grandma can relax in Sunshine City mall (huge, air-conditioned, excellent food courts)
- Evening — dinner in Ikebukuro

> ⚠️ **PokéPark tickets sell out fast.** Go on sale exactly 2 months before your visit date at **6:00pm JST**. Set a phone alarm.

---

## Day 5 — Akihabara + teamLab Borderless 🤖

**Theme:** Tech for dad, immersive art for everyone
**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo

- Slow morning — grandma and kids sleep in / hotel breakfast
- 10:00am — **Dad solo (or family):** Akihabara Electric Town
  - Multi-floor electronics stores, latest Japanese gadgets, robot cafés, anime culture
- 2:00pm — **teamLab Borderless** (Azabudai Hills, Minato)
  - 75+ immersive digital art installations across one floor (grandma-friendly, no stairs between rooms)
  - Kids are spellbound; adults love it; dad geeks out on the tech
  - Allow 2–3 hours
- Evening — Dinner at **Azabudai Hills** complex (excellent restaurant selection)

> ⚠️ **Book timed entry tickets in advance** at teamlab.art — sell out weeks ahead. Kids 4–12: ¥1,500 | Adults: ¥3,800–4,800

---

## Day 6 — Travel to Hakone 🏔️

**Theme:** Scenic journey + onsen arrival
**Stay:** Hakone Kowakien Tenyu (check-in)

- 10:00am — Check out of Hyatt (send luggage ahead via **takkyubin** to Hakone hotel)
- 11:00am — Board **Romancecar** express from Shinjuku (reserved seats, scenic, ~85 min, no transfers — kids love it)
- 1:00pm — Arrive Hakone, transfer to Kowakien Tenyu
- **Afternoon:** Settle in, change into yukata robes
- **Onsen:** Rooftop open-air bath with mountain views; swimsuit option available (great for kids/shy guests)
- **Evening:** Multi-course kaiseki dinner included — beautiful presentation, seasonal ingredients

> 💡 This is grandma's dream day. Included meals, ryokan comfort, no agenda. Pure relaxation.

---

## Day 7 — Hakone: Ropeway + Yunessun 🌋

**Theme:** Volcanic landscape + water park
**Stay:** Hakone Kowakien Tenyu

- Grandma option: **relaxed morning at ryokan** (spa, garden, breakfast at leisure)
- 9:00am — Family: **Hakone Ropeway** — soar over Owakudani volcanic valley, sulfur vents, Mt. Fuji views on clear days
- Continue to **Lake Ashi** — iconic torii gate rising from the water, short boat ride
- 1:00pm — **Hakone Kowakien Yunessun** — onsen water park
  - Slides, cave baths, waterfalls
  - Bathing suits allowed and rentable — perfect for kids
  - Adults get spa relaxation; kids get water park
  - Grandma can join for the gentle warm pools
- 5:00pm — Return to Tenyu for second night's included dinner
- Evening: swap to other onsen side (hotel alternates daily)

---

## Day 8 — Travel to Kyoto + Nishiki Market 🍱

**Theme:** Arrive Kyoto, explore the food scene
**Stay:** Four Seasons Hotel Kyoto (check-in)

- Morning — Breakfast at Tenyu, check out (ship luggage via takkyubin to Four Seasons Kyoto)
- 10:00am — Shinkansen from **Odawara → Kyoto** (~80 min, bullet train)
- 1:00pm — Check into **Four Seasons Kyoto** — 2-bedroom Heritage Garden Residence
  - 800-year-old pond garden, largest indoor pool in Kyoto, world-class spa for grandma
- 3:30pm — **Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's Kitchen")
  - Covered food market, flat and rain-proof, wonderful for all ages
  - Try: pickles, tamagoyaki, fresh tofu, matcha sweets, skewers
- Evening — **Gion district** walk at dusk
  - Lantern-lit streets, willow-lined Shirakawa canal, chance glimpse of maiko/geiko

---

## Day 9 — Temples + Arashiyama 🎋

**Theme:** The soul of Japan
**Stay:** Four Seasons Hotel Kyoto

- 7:00am — **Fushimi Inari Taisha** (go at sunrise — nearly empty)
  - Thousands of torii gates up the mountain
  - Grandma: beautiful lower section is flat and manageable
  - Kids: can run ahead on the trail
- 10:30am — **Kinkaku-ji** (Golden Pavilion) — flat grounds, 30–45 min, unmissable
- 1:30pm — **Arashiyama**
  - Bamboo grove walk
  - Tenryu-ji temple gardens (one of Japan's finest)
  - Riverside lunch
  - Grandma can rest in the garden while kids explore
- Evening — **Kaiseki dinner** near Gion — the meal of the trip

> 💡 Kaiseki tip: book 1–2 months in advance. Ask Four Seasons concierge for recommendations.

---

## Day 10 — Kyoto → Tokyo → Fly Home ✈️

**Theme:** Departure day
**Stay:** None (fly home)

- Morning — Final hotel breakfast, check out
- **Shinkansen Kyoto → Tokyo** (~2h 15min)
- Depending on flight time from Haneda:
  - **Option A:** teamLab Planets (Toyosu) — walk-through water installations, different from Borderless
  - **Option B:** Ginza shopping — department stores, last-minute gifts
  - **Option C:** Head straight to Haneda, last konbini run for snacks/souvenirs
- Fly home from **Haneda (HND)**
