# 🗼 Tokyo — Days 1–5

**Stay:** Hyatt Regency Tokyo, Nishishinjuku (5 nights)
**Address:** 2-7-2 Nishishinjuku, Shinjuku, Tokyo

---

## Why Hyatt Regency Tokyo

- Central Shinjuku location — easy metro access to everything
- Free shuttle bus to Shinjuku Station (great for grandma on tired legs)
- Spacious rooms by Tokyo standards; Mt. Fuji views from upper floors
- Book two **connecting twin rooms** for the group of 6
- Western beds throughout — comfortable for grandma after long days
- 7-Eleven in basement of building for konbini needs

---

## Day 1 — Arrive & Recover

**No agenda. Rest.**

### On Arrival at Haneda (HND)
1. Clear customs and immigration
2. **Get Suica IC cards** for all 6 people at the airport (or top up existing ones)
   - Suica works on nearly all trains, metro, and convenience stores in Japan
   - Load ¥3,000–5,000 per person to start
3. Take **Keikyu Line** (direct from HND to Shinjuku area, ~30 min) or **limousine bus** (more comfortable for grandma and luggage)
4. Check into Hyatt Regency

### Evening
- Short walk around Nishishinjuku
- Konbini dinner (7-Eleven or Lawson in basement) — perfect low-key first night
- **Early bed — aim for 9pm**

> 💡 **Jet lag tip:** Get bright light exposure in the afternoon on arrival day. Avoid napping. This sets your body clock for the rest of the trip.

## Day 2 — Harajuku + MiPig Café 🐷

**Depart hotel:** 10:00am (slow morning)
**Metro:** Shinjuku → Harajuku (JR Yamanote Line, 2 stops, 6 min)

### Morning: Takeshita Street
- Tokyo's most famous kawaii street — colorful, chaotic, absolutely fun for kids
- Try: rainbow cotton candy, crepes, bubble tea, Totti Candy Factory
- Capsule toy machines (gashapon) everywhere — budget ¥500–1,000 per kid
- **Dad option:** Walk 10 min to Omotesando for high design, architecture, flagship stores
- **Grandma option:** Sit at a café on Omotesando with a coffee while kids shop

### Afternoon: MiPig Café 🐷
- **Address:** Near Takeshita Street, Harajuku (confirm on booking)
- **Website:** [mipig.cafe](https://mipig.cafe)
- Book private room session (60 min recommended for a group)
- Cost: ¥3,300/person
- Min age: 5+ (your kids are perfect)
- Micropigs roam freely; kids can hold and feed them
- Even grandma will be delighted

### Late Afternoon
- Walk Omotesando together — tree-lined boulevard, beautiful architecture
- Visit **Nezu Museum** garden for a quiet, beautiful moment (grandma will love this)

### Evening
- Return to Shinjuku for dinner
- Recommended: **Omoide Yokocho** ("Memory Lane") — tiny alleyway of yakitori stalls, incredibly atmospheric
- Or: ramen at **Fuunji** (tsukemen/dipping ramen, near Shinjuku station)

## Day 3 — Tokyo DisneySea 🎡

**Depart hotel:** 8:00am (early start essential)
**Transport:** JR Keiyo Line from Shinjuku → Maihama (~50 min) or taxi (~45 min, more comfortable for grandma)

### Before You Go
- Download **Tokyo Disney Resort App** (essential — ride wait times, Premier Access, food orders)
- Buy tickets in advance at [tokyodisneyresort.jp](https://www.tokyodisneyresort.jp)
- Check **25th Anniversary "Sparkling Jubilee"** special events schedule

### Park Strategy for 6 People

**For kids (7 & 9):**
- Mermaid Lagoon (all indoor, colorful, gentle rides — perfect for young kids)
- Sinbad's Storybook Voyage (relaxing boat ride)
- 20,000 Leagues Under the Sea
- Jumpin' Jellyfish (Mermaid Lagoon)
- Flounder's Flying Fish Coaster

**For adults:**
- Journey to the Center of the Earth (thrilling, must Premier Access)
- Indiana Jones Adventure
- Soaring: Fantastic Flight (Premier Access recommended)
- Tower of Terror (adults only — kids may be too young)

**For grandma (or the whole group together):**
- Mediterranean Harbor — sit, watch the Fantasmic! water show
- Arabian Coast — beautiful theming, gentle attractions, excellent food
- Shows and parades (DisneySea has spectacular live entertainment)
- Simply wandering the gorgeous harbor area — it's like being in Venice

### Tips
- **Premier Access:** Buy through app for Journey to Earth, Soaring, Remy's
- **Wheelchair rental** available at the park entrance — consider for grandma for long days
- **Dietary note:** DisneySea has excellent food, including allergy-friendly options
- Rain ponchos available at the park (June = rainy season, bring light ponchos from home)

## Day 4 — PokéPark KANTO + Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO 🎮

**Depart hotel:** 9:30am
**Transport to Yomiuriland:** Shinjuku → Keio-Yomiuriland Station (~25 min) then gondola lift

### Morning/Afternoon: PokéPark KANTO
- **Website:** [pokepark-kanto.co.jp](https://www.pokepark-kanto.co.jp)
- Opened February 5, 2026 — world's first permanent outdoor Pokémon park
- Located inside Yomiuriland amusement park
- **Areas:**
  - Pokémon Forest — 500m outdoor trail with 600+ Pokémon in their habitats
  - Sedge Town — attractions, parade, shops, Pokémon Gym show (~40 min)
- Plan **3–4 hours minimum**
- Download the PokéPark app in advance to book time slots for areas

**Grandma option for this time:**
- Yomiuriland's observation areas have beautiful panoramic views
- Comfortable seating throughout
- Or: head back to Shinjuku/hotel for rest and meet in Ikebukuro

### Afternoon: Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO
- **Location:** Sunshine City, 3-1-1 Higashi-Ikebukuro, Toshima (Ikebukuro)
- Largest Pokémon Center in Japan — 650 tsubo floor space, 2,500+ items
- **Pikachu Sweets** takeout café inside (seasonal drinks with collectible sleeves)
- **Set a spending budget before entering** (seriously)
- Allow 60–90 minutes

**Grandma (and tired parents) during Pokémon Center:**
- Sunshine City is a massive air-conditioned mall
- Excellent food court, coffee shops, comfortable seating everywhere
- Aqua Park and Sunshine Aquarium also in the complex

### Evening
- Dinner in Ikebukuro — excellent ramen and izakaya options around the station

> ⚠️ **CRITICAL: PokéPark tickets** go on sale exactly **2 months before your visit date at 6:00pm JST.** Set a phone alarm. They sell out within hours.

## Day 5 — Akihabara + teamLab Borderless 🤖

**Theme:** Tech culture + immersive digital art
**Slow morning — depart 10am**

### Morning: Akihabara (Dad's Tech Paradise)
**Transport:** Shinjuku → Akihabara (JR Chuo-Sobu Line, ~20 min)

- **Yodobashi Camera Akiba** — 9 floors of electronics, gadgets, cameras, appliances
- **Sofmap** — specialty tech and electronics
- **Robot Restaurant** area (touristy but kids love it)
- **Retro gaming stores** — the kids may discover their parents' childhoods
- Street-level capsule toy stores — another round of gashapon
- **Grandma option:** Skip Akihabara and rest at hotel or explore nearby **Ueno Park** (beautiful, flat, relaxed)

### Afternoon: teamLab Borderless
**Location:** Azabudai Hills Garden Plaza B1, 5-9-9 Toranomon, Minato
**Transport from Akihabara:** ~25 min by metro
**Website:** [teamlab.art](https://www.teamlab.art/jp/e/tokyo/)

- 75+ immersive digital art installations — all on ONE floor (grandma-friendly!)
- Artworks move and flow between rooms — no map, no path, explore freely
- Allow **2–3 hours**
- Kids are absolutely spellbound (interactive light rooms, crystal universe, etc.)
- Dad will geek out on the technology behind it
- **Book timed entry in advance** — sells out weeks ahead

**Ticket prices:**
| Age | Price |
|-----|-------|
| 4–12 | ¥1,500 |
| 13–18 | ¥2,800 |
| Adult (weekday) | ¥3,800 |
| Adult (weekend) | ¥4,800 |

### Evening
- Dinner at **Azabudai Hills** complex — excellent and varied restaurant selection
- The complex itself is stunning modern architecture worth exploring

## Tokyo Practical Notes

### Getting Around
- Use **Suica cards** for all metro and JR train travel
- **Taxi:** More expensive but essential for grandma on long days or bad weather. All major taxis accept IC cards.
- **Hotel shuttle:** Hyatt Regency has a free shuttle to Shinjuku Station — use it!

### Neighborhoods at a Glance
| Neighborhood | Why Go |
|---|---|
| Shinjuku | Home base, nightlife, ramen, shopping |
| Harajuku | Kawaii culture, kids' shopping, Omotesando |
| Ikebukuro | Pokémon Center, Sunshine City, department stores |
| Akihabara | Electronics, anime, tech, retro games |
| Azabudai | teamLab, modern Tokyo, upscale dining |

### Grandma Comfort Tips
- All major Tokyo metro stations have elevators — use the **barrier-free route** option on Google Maps
- Convenience stores (konbini) are excellent rest stops — clean toilets, chairs, food, AC
- Don't over-schedule — two or three activities per day maximum
- The Hyatt concierge can arrange private car transfers for any day that feels too tiring for trains

### Food Must-Tries in Tokyo
- Ramen (try different styles: shoyu, miso, tonkotsu, tsukemen)
- Conveyor belt sushi (kaiten-zushi) — great for kids
- Onigiri from konbini (surprisingly amazing)
- Tamagoyaki (rolled egg) from Tsukiji Outer Market
- Fluffy Japanese pancakes (soufflé pancakes)
- Wagyu beef — splurge at least once
