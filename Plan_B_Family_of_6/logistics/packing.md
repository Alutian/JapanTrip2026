# 🧳 Packing List

**Group:** 2 adults + 2 kids (ages 7 & 9) + grandma (75)
**Duration:** 10 days | **Season:** Early June (warm, humid, rainy season)
**Strategy:** Pack light — Japan has excellent laundry facilities and konbini for anything forgotten

---

## Bag Strategy

### Recommended Setup for 6 People
- **2 large checked bags** (27–29") — shared between the family
- **1 medium checked bag** (24") — for grandma
- **2 carry-on bags** — one per adult couple (backpacks)
- **1 small day pack** per adult — for daily use at sights

Use **takkyubin** to forward large bags between cities — you won't need to lug them on trains.

## Money & Payments

### Cash (Still Important in Japan)
- Japan is more cash-friendly than most developed countries
- ATMs: **7-Eleven ATMs** and Japan Post ATMs reliably accept foreign cards
- Bring ~$200–300 USD equivalent in yen, replenish at 7-Eleven ATMs
- Notify your bank of Japan travel before departure

### Cards
- **Credit cards** accepted at most hotels, department stores, and larger restaurants
- **IC cards (Suica)** — loaded with yen, used like a transit/payment card
- Smaller restaurants, temples, and market stalls: **cash only**

## What NOT to Pack (Buy in Japan Instead)

Japan has excellent and cheap versions of these — save the bag space:
- Sunscreen (Japanese SPF products are superior)
- Umbrellas (¥500 at any konbini)
- Rain ponchos (available everywhere)
- Snacks (konbini is a food paradise)
- Tissues / hand towels (distributed free everywhere)
- Tote bags (every shop gives beautiful ones)
