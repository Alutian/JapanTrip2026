# 🚄 Transport in Japan

Everything you need to know about getting around with 6 people, including grandma.

---

## The Essential Cards & Passes

### 1. Suica IC Card (Everyone Gets One)
- **What it is:** Rechargeable IC card — works on nearly all trains, metro, buses, and convenience stores
- **Get them:** At Haneda Airport vending machines on arrival day (or at any major JR station)
- **Load:** ¥3,000–5,000 per person to start; top up at any station vending machine
- **Who needs one:** All 6 people, including grandma and kids
- Kids under 12 can get a **child Suica** (half-price fares)

### 2. JR Pass (Buy Before Leaving the US)
- **What it is:** Unlimited travel on JR (Japan Rail) Shinkansen and trains nationwide
- **Buy:** Must purchase before arriving in Japan at [japanrailpass.net](https://www.japanrailpass.net)
- **Which pass:** **7-day JR Pass** — covers the Shinkansen segments of your trip
- **Cost:** ~$330 USD/adult, ~$165 USD/child (under 12)
  - Total for group: ~$990–1,320 depending on grandma/adult mix
- **Covers:**
  - ✅ SFO → HND (no, flights not covered)
  - ✅ Odawara → Kyoto (Shinkansen)
  - ✅ Kyoto → Tokyo (Shinkansen)
  - ✅ Tokyo area JR trains
  - ❌ Does NOT cover Tokyo metro (use Suica)
  - ❌ Does NOT cover Odakyu Romancecar to Hakone (buy separately)

> 💡 **Activate the JR Pass on Day 1 or Day 6** (whichever makes sense based on your first JR train). It covers 7 consecutive days from activation.

## Getting Around Tokyo

### Tokyo Metro & Trains
- **Suica card** covers all metro and most JR trains in Tokyo
- Google Maps is excellent for routing — select "transit" mode
- Most major stations have **elevators** — use Google Maps "accessible route" for grandma
- Avoid rush hour (7:30–9:30am, 5:30–8pm) with young kids and grandma — crowded and hot

### Key Tokyo Lines You'll Use
| Line | Color | Used For |
|------|-------|---------|
| JR Yamanote Line | Green | Shinjuku → Harajuku → Ikebukuro loop |
| JR Chuo-Sobu | Yellow | Shinjuku → Akihabara |
| JR Keiyo | Red/Orange | Tokyo → Maihama (DisneySea) |
| Keio/Odakyu | Various | Shinjuku → Yomiuriland (PokéPark) |
| Tokyo Metro | Multiple | General city travel |

### Taxis in Tokyo
- Safe, clean, and metered
- All taxis accept IC cards (Suica) and most accept credit cards
- **When to use taxis:**
  - With grandma when she's tired
  - Rainy days
  - Late at night
  - When carrying lots of shopping
- App: **GO** (Japan's biggest taxi app) — book in English, pay by card

### Hyatt Regency Hotel Shuttle
- Free shuttle bus between hotel and Shinjuku Station
- Use it! Saves grandma the 8-min walk, especially with bags

## Tokyo → Hakone

### Option 1: Romancecar Express ⭐ Recommended
- **Route:** Shinjuku → Hakone-Yumoto (direct, no transfers)
- **Duration:** ~85 minutes
- **Cost:** ~¥1,270/person + ~¥900 limited express surcharge
- **Book:** [odakyu.jp](https://www.odakyu.jp/english/romancecar/) — reserve seats in advance
- Scenic, comfortable, reserved seats — kids and grandma love this
- From Hakone-Yumoto, take a short taxi or Kowakien hotel shuttle to Tenyu

### Option 2: Shinkansen to Odawara + Local Train
- **Route:** Shinjuku/Tokyo → Odawara (Shinkansen, ~35 min) → local to Hakone
- Covered by JR Pass to Odawara
- Faster total time but more transfers

## Hakone → Kyoto

### Shinkansen from Odawara
- **Route:** Odawara → Kyoto (Nozomi or Hikari Shinkansen)
- **Duration:** ~70–80 minutes
- **Book reserved seats** in advance — secure a 6-person block
- Covered by JR Pass (Odawara is a JR station)
- From Kyoto Station: taxi or private car to Four Seasons (~15 min)

## Getting Around Kyoto

### Taxis (Best for This Group)
- **Most practical for a group of 6 with grandma**
- Flat-ish city for most tourist areas — taxis are fast and cheap
- Rarely more than ¥1,500–3,000 between major sights
- Apps: **GO** works in Kyoto too

### Key Kyoto Trains
| Route | Line | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Kyoto Station → Fushimi Inari | JR Nara Line | 5 min — station is literally at the shrine gate |
| Kyoto Station → Arashiyama | JR San-in Line → Saga-Arashiyama | 15 min |
| Nishiki Market area | Walk from Kawaramachi | 5-min walk from Hankyu station |

### City Buses
- Comprehensive bus network covers all major sights
- Get crowded in peak tourist times
- Not ideal for grandma at rush times
- Day pass available: ¥700/day unlimited

## Luggage Forwarding (Takkyubin) 🧳

**The secret weapon for multi-city travel in Japan**

### What Is It?
Yamato Transport's door-to-door luggage delivery service. Send bags between hotels overnight so you travel on Shinkansen hands-free.

### How It Works
1. The night before (or morning of) travel, bring bags to hotel front desk
2. Fill out a forwarding slip (hotel staff help with this)
3. Pay per bag
4. Bags arrive at next hotel by next day

### Cost
- ~¥1,500–2,500 per bag depending on size
- For 6 people with ~6 bags: ~¥9,000–15,000 per transfer
- Worth every yen — especially for grandma

### When to Use It
| Transfer | When to Send |
|----------|-------------|
| Tokyo → Hakone | Morning of Day 6 |
| Hakone → Kyoto | Morning of Day 8 |
| Kyoto → Haneda | Day 10 (luggage to hotel near airport, or leave at station) |

### Important Notes
- Send bags 1–2 days in advance if possible (next-day is standard, same-day rare)
- Confirm hotel accepts incoming luggage (all major hotels do)
- Keep a small day bag with 1–2 nights' essentials when sending bags ahead

## Accessibility Notes for Grandma

### Tokyo Metro
- All major stations on tourist routes have elevators
- Search "barrier-free route" in Google Maps or Hyperdia
- Station staff are helpful — wave for assistance, they will come

### Shinkansen
- Reserved seats — book aisle seats for grandma
- Wide aisles, clean accessible toilets on board
- Stations have elevators and escalators

### Kyoto
- Taxis are the most comfortable option
- Most major temple grounds are walkable but have **gravel paths** — comfortable shoes essential
- Fushimi Inari lower section: flat ✅
- Kinkaku-ji: flat ✅
- Arashiyama bamboo grove: flat ✅
- **Skip:** Kiyomizudera Temple (steep stone stairs), Mt. Inari full hike

### General Tips
- **Folding cane** — pack one for grandma for uneven temple paths
- **Comfortable flat shoes** — Japan involves a LOT of walking
- **Never rush connections** — build extra time at stations
- If ever unsure, ask station staff — Japanese rail staff are extraordinarily helpful
