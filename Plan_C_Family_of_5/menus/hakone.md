# Hakone Menu

Hakone is mostly locked to the ryokan's rhythm — kaiseki dinners and onsen sessions are scheduled by the hotel. Daytime has flexibility; here's the menu.

---

## 🌅 Morning / Daytime Blocks

### H1 · Classic Hakone Loop ⭐
- **Route:** Gora → Hakone Tozan Cable Car → **Hakone Ropeway** (over Owakudani volcanic valley, sulfur steam, possible Mt. Fuji views) → Togendai → **Lake Ashi pirate ship cruise** to Hakone-machi → bus back to Gora
- **Duration:** 4–5h. The essential Hakone day.
- **Food:** **Kuro-tamago** (black eggs boiled in volcanic springs at Owakudani — legend says 7 extra years per egg). Lakeside soba at **Hatsuhana Honten** near Hakone-Yumoto for lunch.
- **Vibe:** Day 6 morning-to-afternoon. Everyone does this.

### H2 · Hakone Open Air Museum
- **Area:** Chōkoku-no-Mori station (2 stops from Gora)
- **Activity:** 70,000 sqm outdoor sculpture park, Picasso pavilion (300+ works), foot spa with view, **giant net sculpture for kids to climb in** (Symphonic Sculpture). Grandma-friendly paths.
- **Duration:** ~2h
- **Food:** Café on-site is fine; better option is **Gora Brewery & Grill** 10 min away for local microbrew + grilled Kanagawa pork.
- **Vibe:** Day 6 afternoon after H1, or Day 7 morning pre-checkout.

### H3 · Yunessun Water Park
- **Area:** 5-min walk from Kowakien area
- **Activity:** Onsen-meets-water-park. Swimsuit-allowed baths: wine bath, green tea bath, coffee bath (themed, kid-friendly). Slides. Separate traditional nude onsen side for adults.
- **Duration:** 2–3h
- **Food:** On-site cafeteria is fine; not a food destination day.
- **Vibe:** Alternative to H2 for a wetter afternoon. Kids will rate this higher than the sculpture park.

### H4 · Gora Park + Pola Museum
- **Area:** Gora
- **Activity:** **Gora Park** — French-style garden, glassblowing workshop (kids can make a souvenir ~¥2,500). Then **Pola Museum of Art** — world-class Impressionist collection in a forest setting, bus from Gora.
- **Duration:** 3h combined
- **Food:** **Itoh Dining by Nobu** — Hakone beef steak, splurge lunch. OR casual **Gora Hanaori** lunch set.
- **Vibe:** If you want a calmer Day 6 or swap-in alternative.

## 🍽️ Food Notes

Most meals are ryokan kaiseki (locked). For the one daytime meal not at the ryokan:

- **Hatsuhana Honten** — handmade soba in Hakone-Yumoto, since 1934, thatched roof building, cash only. Queue but worth it.
- **Gora Brewery & Grill** — casual, microbrew flight, local pork, kid-friendly seating.
- **Amazake-chaya** — 400-year-old tea house on the old Tokaido trail, thatched roof, amazake (sweet sake, non-alcoholic), mochi. Near Hakone-machi after the cruise.

---

## 🚫 Skip

- **Hakone Shrine** on Lake Ashi — the famous red torii gate *in* the water is one photo; the shrine itself is contemplative-style (= boring per stated preference). The boat passes it — photo from the deck is enough.
- **Odawara Castle** — it's a 1960s concrete reconstruction, not worth a detour.
