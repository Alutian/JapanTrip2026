# Kyoto Menu

3 nights in Kyoto, used as base for 2 day trips (Nara, Osaka). Locked items: Nintendo Museum (lottery), kaiseki dinner (book via hotel concierge).

---

## 🌅 Kyoto Morning Blocks

### K1 · Fushimi Inari Sunrise ⭐
- **Area:** Fushimi (5 min by JR from Kyoto Station)
- **Activity:** Get there by **6:30am** for near-empty red torii tunnels. Lower loop (30–45 min, flat) is grandma-manageable. Family can go higher to the Yotsutsuji viewpoint (~45 min further, 233m elevation) for the city view.
- **Duration:** 2h including transit
- **Food:** **Vermillion Café** at the base of the mountain — espresso + breakfast, or **Inari-sushi** (pouches of sweet tofu stuffed with rice) from stalls in the approach. *Kitsune udon* at any shop in the area (the fox spirit Inari's favorite food).
- **Vibe:** Must-do. Day 8 morning.

### K2 · Arashiyama Bamboo + Monkey Park
- **Area:** Arashiyama (15 min JR from Kyoto)
- **Activity:** **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** (20 min walk-through, get there by 8am before crowds) → **Togetsukyo Bridge** riverside → **Iwatayama Monkey Park** (wild macaques, *20-min uphill hike* — grandma sits at the base, family climbs). OR skip monkeys and do **Tenryu-ji garden** (skippable if garden-fatigued).
- **Duration:** 3–4h
- **Food:** **Yoshida-ya** (unagi, since 1868, tiny counter, cash only) OR **Arashiyama Yoshimura** for handmade soba with riverside view.
- **Vibe:** Alternative Day 8 morning if the family wants more variety than Fushimi alone.

### K3 · Nintendo Museum ⭐ (LOCKED)
- **Area:** Uji (20 min south of Kyoto Station)
- **Activity:** 2-floor museum of Nintendo's entire history — playing cards (1889), Hanafuda, Game Boys, NES, every console ever, interactive game zones with oversized controllers. Pre-booked timed entry only.
- **Duration:** 2.5–3h
- **Food:** **Uji is the matcha capital.** Lunch at **Itoh Kyuemon** for matcha soba + matcha parfait. Or **Tsuen Tea** (oldest tea shop in the world, 1160) for a tea flight.
- **Vibe:** Day 8 afternoon. Must be booked via lottery 3 months out.

### K4 · Kyoto Imperial Palace + Nishiki
- **Area:** Central Kyoto
- **Activity:** **Kyoto Imperial Palace** grounds (free, reservation-free since 2016, walk the grounds not the interior — huge park, few tourists, grandma-friendly) → **Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's Kitchen," 400m covered food arcade)
- **Duration:** 3h
- **Food:** Graze Nishiki: **Aritsugu** knife shop (watch masters sharpen), **Mochitsuki Ya** fresh mochi, **Kai** tamagoyaki (thick omelette on a stick), **Tako Tamago** octopus with quail egg, **Sawawa** warabi-mochi for dessert.
- **Vibe:** Flexible afternoon / evening.

## 🦌 Day Trip: Nara (locked for Day 9)

### N1 · Nara Park + Todai-ji
- **Transit:** JR Miyakoji Rapid, Kyoto Station → Nara Station, 45 min, ¥720. Use Suica.
- **Activity:** **Nara Park** (semi-wild sika deer that bow for shika-senbei crackers, ¥200/pack — the bow is a trained trick, kids will lose their minds) → **Todai-ji Daibutsu-den** (world's largest wooden building, 15m bronze Buddha — 30 min, wow-scale not contemplative) → **Kasuga Taisha** (3,000 stone lanterns, skippable if shrine-fatigued) → stroll **Naramachi** (old merchant district).
- **Duration:** 5–6h including transit
- **Food:** **Harishin** — kakinoha-zushi (mackerel sushi wrapped in persimmon leaves, Nara specialty, preserved since before refrigeration). OR **Nakatanidou** for the famous mochi-pounding show + fresh yomogi mochi (tourist-known but legit — the pounding happens every 20 min in front of you).
- **Vibe:** Day 9 half-to-full day. Back to Kyoto by late afternoon for kaiseki dinner.

## 🍜 Day Trip: Osaka (removed from Day 10 per decisions.md #13)

Kept here as an optional pick — if Day 8 afternoon frees up or you want a bonus dinner out, Osaka is only 15 min away.

### O1 · Dotonbori Food Crawl
- **Transit:** Shinkansen Kyoto → Shin-Osaka (15 min) + Midosuji Line to Namba (10 min). Or regular JR Kyoto Line (~45 min cheaper).
- **Activity:** **Dotonbori** canal-side food street — giant mechanical crab at **Kani Doraku**, takoyaki, kushikatsu, okonomiyaki. **Kuromon Ichiba Market** nearby for daytime grazing.
- **Food highlights (avoid chain versions):**
  - **Takoyaki Wanaka** — Namba branch, since 1947, crispy-outside takoyaki
  - **Kushikatsu Daruma** Shinsekai branch — since 1929, NEVER double-dip the sauce (it's shared)
  - **Mizuno** okonomiyaki — tiny counter, since 1945, they'll cook it for you
  - **Ichiran ramen** Dotonbori branch — always open, 24h, kid booths
- **Vibe:** Optional extra evening if energy allows. Add ~4h round-trip.

## 🌃 Kyoto Evening Blocks

### KE1 · Gion at Dusk ⭐
- **Area:** Gion / Pontocho
- **Activity:** **Hanamikoji Street** (stone-paved, wooden ochaya teahouses, possible maiko spotting between 5:45–6:15pm) → **Shirakawa Canal** (willow-lined, lantern-lit, most atmospheric 10 minutes in Japan) → **Pontocho Alley** across the river (narrow lantern-lit restaurant alley)
- **Food:** **Izuju** (saba-zushi, pressed mackerel sushi since 1912, tiny counter in Gion, take-away or eat-in). For kaiseki splurge: **Kikunoi Roan** (Michelin 2-star kaiseki, book via Four Seasons concierge 2 months out).
- **Vibe:** Day 8 evening after Nintendo Museum.

### KE2 · Kaiseki Farewell Dinner (LOCKED)
- Book via Kyoto hotel concierge. Pontocho kaiseki spots: **Kikunoi Honten**, **Kiyamachi Sakuragawa**, **Giro Giro Hitoshina** (edgier, more affordable ~¥6,000).
- **Vibe:** Day 9 evening after Nara. The trip's nicest meal.

### KE3 · Kyoto Ramen Koji (casual night)
- **Area:** Kyoto Station 10F
- **Activity:** 9 ramen shops representing different regional styles (Sapporo, Hakata, Tokyo...) all on one floor. Order by ticket machine, ~10 min wait, ¥1,000/bowl. Kid-friendly, grandma-friendly.
- **Vibe:** Easy-night fallback if kaiseki got moved or you want a no-plan evening.

## 🚫 Skip

- **Kiyomizudera** — we cut contemplative temples
- **Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)** — 20 min of staring at a building from a fixed viewing spot. Photo exists online.
- **Ginkakuji** and **Philosopher's Path** — shrine + walk, both fall in the cut category
- **"Kimono rental" walking tours** — fine if the kids want it, but expect heat in June
- **Maiko show "performances"** aimed at tourists — the real maiko are in Gion doing their job, spotting one naturally is the experience
