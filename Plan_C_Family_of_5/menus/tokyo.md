# Tokyo Menu — Flexible Blocks

Mix and match across 4 Tokyo days. **Locked blocks** (teamLab Borderless, MiPig Café, Romancecar out) are in [../itinerary.md](../itinerary.md); this file is for the flexible slots.

---

## 🌅 Morning Blocks (active)

### M1 · Harajuku Kawaii Morning
- **Area:** Harajuku / Omotesando
- **Activity:** Takeshita Street (crepes, kawaii shops, capsule toys) → MiPig Café (60-min pre-booked private room) → Omotesando architecture walk (~10 min stroll to lunch)
- **Duration:** ~3h
- **Food:** **Maisen Aoyama** — tonkatsu institution since 1965, housed in an old bathhouse, Japanese-staff mostly, pork is the star. Skip the Gonpachi "Kill Bill" restaurant 2 blocks over (pure tourist trap).
- **Vibe:** Good Day 2 opener. Note: MiPig is locked to a booked slot.

### M2 · Tsukiji Outer Market Food Crawl
- **Area:** Tsukiji (the inner fish market moved to Toyosu; outer market is still great)
- **Activity:** Graze sashimi, tamagoyaki, uni bowls, fresh seafood skewers, matcha sweets across 50+ stalls. Optional walk to **Hamarikyu Gardens** (tea house on an island — skippable if garden-fatigued).
- **Duration:** ~2.5h
- **Food:** **Sushi-dai** (if you want the splurge queue — 1h+ wait, worth it) OR **Uogashi Nihon-ichi** standing sushi inside the market (cheap, fast, amazing, locals only). Budget ¥1,500–3,000/person at stalls vs ¥6,000 at Sushi-dai.
- **Vibe:** Best on a day you want food *to be* the activity. Get there by 9am before stalls sell out.

### M3 · Asakusa Old-Tokyo Morning
- **Area:** Asakusa
- **Activity:** Kaminarimon gate + **Nakamise Street** (300m of traditional craft shops, grilled senbei, ningyo-yaki) → Sensoji briefly (5-min glance, the street is the draw) → **Tokyo Skytree** observation deck (350m, tallest tower in Japan; Sumida Aquarium below if time)
- **Duration:** ~3–4h
- **Food:** **Asakusa Imahan** — sukiyaki institution since 1895, old-school private tatami rooms, premium wagyu. OR **Daikokuya Tempura** (since 1887, still cooking in sesame oil, massive ebi-ten on rice).
- **Vibe:** Strong Day 3 option. Nakamise is shopping-heavy not temple-heavy, so it dodges your "boring shrine" concern.

### M4 · Ghibli Museum + Kichijoji
- **Area:** Mitaka (30 min from Shinjuku)
- **Activity:** **Ghibli Museum** (2h, no re-entry, camera-free inside — the point is to experience it). Then walk 15 min through **Inokashira Park** (paddle boats, pond) to **Kichijoji** — leafy residential hipster neighborhood.
- **Duration:** ~4h including transit
- **Food:** **Harmonica Yokocho** in Kichijoji — narrow lanes of tiny izakayas and food stalls, locals-only vibe. Try **Iseya** yakitori (since 1928, smokey, cash only, no English menu, ~¥200/skewer).
- **Vibe:** Alternative Day 3 if kids love Ghibli films. Ticket lottery opens monthly 1st at 10am JST — must apply 2+ months ahead.

### M5 · Akihabara Geek Morning
- **Area:** Akihabara
- **Activity:** Multi-floor electronics + retro gaming at **Super Potato** (5 floors of nostalgia), **Mandarake Complex** (8 floors of manga/anime collectibles), **Yodobashi Camera** (the one store to end all electronics stores). Gachapon halls.
- **Duration:** ~3h (endless if Ajay goes solo)
- **Food:** **Kanda Matsuya** (soba since 1884, 10-min walk, wood-plank interior, ~¥1,000/bowl, locals-only). OR **CoCo Ichibanya curry** for kids (chain but consistent).
- **Vibe:** Day 5 morning before Romancecar to Hakone. Grandma can skip and rest at hotel lounge.

### M6 · Odaiba Science Day
- **Area:** Odaiba (artificial island in Tokyo Bay)
- **Activity:** **Miraikan** (National Museum of Emerging Science) — hands-on robotics, ASIMO demos, space simulators. Then life-sized moving **Unicorn Gundam statue** at DiverCity (transforms every 30 min, 3pm–9pm).
- **Duration:** ~4h
- **Food:** **Yokocho restaurants at Aqua City mall** — replica of an old Tokyo food alley with tonkatsu, ramen, tempura counters. Not gourmet but solid and atmospheric.
- **Vibe:** Backup Day 3 option. Good rainy-day plan.

## ☕ Afternoon Blocks (slow)

These are low-energy filler for when the morning was big or jet-lag is lingering.

### A1 · Hotel Rest + Neighborhood Café
- Go back to the hotel, let kids nap or play, grandma spa if available.
- **Blue Bottle** branches in Shinjuku/Shibuya/Aoyama are the default good coffee stop.

### A2 · Depachika Picnic
- **Isetan Shinjuku** or **Mitsukoshi Ginza** basement food halls — the most beautiful food halls in the world. Pick up bento, fruit, wagashi, premium prepared foods. Take back to hotel or to a nearby park (Shinjuku Chuo Park, Hamarikyu).

### A3 · Yanaka Old-Town Walk
- **Yanaka Ginza** shopping street — Tokyo as it was 60 years ago. Wooden shops, cat-themed everything (the area has a cat population), tiny snack shops. Very grandma-friendly, ~1h amble.
- **Food:** **Kayaba Coffee** — 1938 kissaten (old-style café), egg sandwiches, siphon coffee.

### A4 · Ginza Window Shopping
- Chuo-dori pedestrian-only on weekends (Hokōsha Tengoku). Itoya stationery (9 floors), Uniqlo flagship, Muji flagship. Saves kid energy for evening.

## 🌃 Evening Blocks (interesting)

### E1 · Shibuya Neon Night ⭐
- **Area:** Shibuya
- **Activity:** **Pokémon Center Shibuya** + **Nintendo Tokyo** (Parco) → **Shibuya Sky** observation deck at sunset (timed entry, book 1 week out) → **Shibuya Scramble crossing** from above, then from the ground after dark
- **Food:** **Kaikaya by the Sea** — seafood izakaya, local institution, English menu but Japanese clientele, reservations needed. OR **Gyopao Gyoza** for kid-friendly.
- **Vibe:** One of the essential Tokyo experiences. Pair with M1 on Day 2.

### E2 · Shinjuku Yakitori Night
- **Area:** Shinjuku (west side)
- **Activity:** **Omoide Yokocho** ("Memory Lane," nicknamed Piss Alley) — narrow post-war yakitori alley, smoky, atmospheric → **Golden Gai** adults-only peek (6 alleys of tiny 5-seat bars, no kids after 8pm)
- **Food:** Any yakitori counter in Omoide Yokocho — **Asadachi** for adventurous (raw liver, pig testicles) or **Uchu** for standard skewers. **Fuunji** 5-min walk for tsukemen dipping noodles post-yakitori.
- **Vibe:** Day 4 evening. Kids will like the alley energy; adults will want to come back without them.

### E3 · Ikebukuro Pokémon Night
- **Area:** Ikebukuro (Sunshine City complex)
- **Activity:** **Pokémon Center MEGA TOKYO** (largest in Japan, 2,500+ items, Pikachu Sweets Café) → **Sunshine City Sky Circus** observation deck (VR/AR enhanced, weirder than Shibuya Sky) → Sunshine Aquarium if time
- **Food:** **Mutekiya** — legendary Ikebukuro ramen, rich tonkotsu, always a queue but moves fast. Iconic spot, locals + tourists both.
- **Vibe:** This replaces the need for PokéPark. Day 3 evening after whichever morning block you pick.

### E4 · Ginza Depachika + Sushi
- **Area:** Ginza
- **Activity:** Walk Chuo-dori, Itoya stationery, depachika food hall as entertainment
- **Food:** **Kyubey Ginza** (omakase sushi legend, splurge — reserve weeks ahead) OR **Rokurinsha** at Tokyo Ramen Street underground (tsukemen, 1h queue, worth it). For kids/grandma: **Ten Ichi** tempura.
- **Vibe:** If you want one "adult dinner" night in Tokyo.

### E5 · Asakusa River + Skytree Sunset
- **Area:** Sumida River / Asakusa
- **Activity:** **Tokyo Water Bus** cruise from Hamarikyu → Asakusa (45 min on the river, great for kids) at sunset → walk along Sumida River with Skytree illuminated
- **Food:** **Asakusa Sometaro** okonomiyaki — family-style, cook at your table, 1937 wooden building, floor seating. Very authentic.
- **Vibe:** Alternative evening if you did M3 Asakusa morning elsewhere or skipped it.

## 🧭 Tokyo Neighborhood Quick Reference

| Neighborhood | Character | Good for |
|--------------|-----------|----------|
| **Shibuya** | Neon, youth, crossing | Evenings, observation decks |
| **Shinjuku** | Everything at once, biggest station | Hotels, yakitori alleys, Gyoen (skipping), Ramen Street |
| **Harajuku / Omotesando** | Kawaii + luxury in the same 10 blocks | MiPig morning, tonkatsu lunch |
| **Ginza** | Upscale, food halls, architecture | Afternoons, adult dinner |
| **Asakusa** | Old Tokyo, Skytree adjacent | Morning or sunset |
| **Ikebukuro** | Anime, Pokémon, Sunshine City | One evening, done |
| **Akihabara** | Electronics, retro gaming, anime | Solo Ajay morning or quick family stop |
| **Odaiba** | Artificial island, malls, science museum | Rainy day fallback |
| **Tsukiji** | Food market (outer) | Breakfast-focused morning |
| **Kichijoji / Yanaka** | Quiet, lived-in, old-Tokyo | Slow afternoons |

## 🚫 Tourist Traps to Skip

- **Robot Restaurant** — closed, don't believe outdated blogs
- **Gonpachi "Kill Bill" restaurant** in Nishi-Azabu — mid food, pure tourist fame
- **Character cafés on main Harajuku strip** (except MiPig which is legit)
- **Ichiran at Shibuya Scramble Square** — long queue for mediocre ramen; branches elsewhere have same food no queue
- **Themed izakayas in Kabukicho** aimed at tourists (ninja, prison, Alice in Wonderland) — novelty > food
