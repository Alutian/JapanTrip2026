## Dinner Plan (no meals included)

The biggest tradeoff vs a ryokan pick. Mitigation: book **one dinner-only kaiseki** at a nearby ryokan/hotel for one of the two nights so the family still gets the Hakone kaiseki experience.

- **Fujiya Hotel Main Dining Room "The Fujiya"** — accepts outside dinner reservations. French + Japanese courses in the historic 1878 dining hall. Book ~1 month out. Dress code: smart casual (no shorts/sandals at dinner).
- **Alternative:** Gora Hanaougi restaurant, Hakone Tensui dinner plan, or a local Gora/Kowakudani izakaya for a less formal night.
- **Other night:** self-catered in the unit (kitchen), konbini haul, or Yunessun's in-park restaurants.

## Transport from the Property

- **Nearest station:** Kowakudani (Hakone Tozan Railway) — confirm walking time on arrival
- **Hakone Free Pass** covers all Tozan line + Ropeway + Lake Ashi boat + cable car + Tozan Bus. Buy 2-day pass at Hakone-Yumoto Station on arrival (~¥6,100/adult).
- **Classic Loop (Day 6):** Kowakudani → Gora (Tozan) → Ropeway → Owakudani → Togendai → Lake Ashi boat → Moto-Hakone → bus back. All Free-Pass covered.
